# DAILY BRIEF STANDARD

## Purpose

This file defines the required output standard for the BiH Daily Topic Monitor.

The Daily Topic Monitor produces one daily briefing focused on explicitly registered topics in `MONITORED_TOPICS.md`.

The product is not a news digest.

It is an analytical daily monitoring brief that answers:

- What happened today?
- Which topic does it affect?
- What is genuinely new?
- Does it indicate escalation, persistence, de-escalation, or insufficient evidence?
- What is the significance?
- What should be watched next?

The briefing must be written in Bulgarian.

Technical identifiers, topic IDs, confidence levels, and classification labels may remain in English.

---

# Daily brief identity

Each daily briefing must have a unique ID:

`DBRIEF-YYYYMMDD`

Example:

`DBRIEF-20260709`

The briefing must include:

- DATE
- MONITORING WINDOW
- TOPICS REVIEWED
- OVERALL DAILY ASSESSMENT
- CONFIDENCE

---

# Required structure

Every daily briefing must use the following structure.

---

## 1. HEADER

Required format:

    DAILY TOPIC BRIEF

    BRIEF ID:
    DBRIEF-YYYYMMDD

    DATE:
    YYYY-MM-DD

    MONITORING WINDOW:
    YYYY-MM-DD 00:00–23:59 local time

    OUTPUT LANGUAGE:
    Bulgarian

    TOPICS REVIEWED:
    [list of TOPIC IDs]

    OVERALL DAILY ASSESSMENT:
    ESCALATION / LIMITED ESCALATION / STABLE TENSION / DE-ESCALATION / MIXED / INSUFFICIENT DATA

    CONFIDENCE:
    LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH

---

## 2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Executive Summary must be a short analytical synthesis, not a list.

Length:

- normally 120–180 words;
- maximum 220 words.

It must answer:

- What is the most important daily picture?
- Did the day show escalation, stabilization, or insufficient evidence?
- Which topics were most active?
- Were there any cross-topic links?
- What is the main risk to watch tomorrow?

Do not exaggerate.

Do not claim escalation if evidence shows only rhetoric or repeated reporting.

---

## 3. TOPIC SECTIONS

Each registered topic must be reviewed.

Use this format for every topic:

    ## [TOPIC ID] — [TOPIC NAME]

    DAILY STATUS:
    NO SIGNIFICANT DEVELOPMENT / NEW DEVELOPMENT / UPDATE / MULTIPLE DEVELOPMENTS / UNCONFIRMED SIGNALS ONLY

    DAILY ASSESSMENT:
    ESCALATING / LIMITED ESCALATION / STABLE / DE-ESCALATING / MIXED / INSUFFICIENT DATA

    SIGNIFICANCE:
    LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL

    CONFIDENCE:
    LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH

Then write the following subsections.

### Key developments

If there are meaningful developments, summarize them in concise analytical prose.

If there are no meaningful developments, write exactly:

`Няма съществено ново развитие.`

### What is genuinely new

Explain what changed compared with the previous known situation.

If the item is only repeated reporting, say so and do not treat it as a new development.

### Analytical significance

Explain why the development matters for the topic.

This should answer:

- Does it increase risk?
- Does it confirm an existing pattern?
- Does it contradict a previous assumption?
- Does it broaden the issue geographically or institutionally?
- Does it show movement from rhetoric to action?
- Does it reveal coordination?

### What to watch next

List 2–4 concrete indicators.

These indicators must be specific and observable.

Bad:

- “Да се следи ситуацията.”

Good:

- “Дали полицията ще квалифицира инцидента като етнически мотивиран.”
- “Дали същият наратив ще се появи синхронизирано в други канали.”
- “Дали ще има реакция от EUFOR, OHR или държавни институции.”

---

# Topic-specific requirements

## Tension escalation

For `TOP-BIH-TENSION-ESCALATION`, the monitor must distinguish:

- normal political conflict;
- institutional obstruction;
- constitutional crisis indicators;
- mobilization;
- direct security relevance.

Do not classify routine political rhetoric as escalation unless it is connected to action, institutional consequence, mobilization, or credible warning.

---

## Radical and paramilitary activity

For `TOP-BIH-RADICAL-PARAMILITARY-ACTIVITY`, the monitor must distinguish:

- rhetoric;
- symbolic activity;
- organizational activity;
- recruitment;
- training;
- weapons;
- operational activity;
- official investigation.

Do not label a group as paramilitary, extremist, or violent without evidence.

Use careful language:

- “reported”
- “alleged”
- “unconfirmed”
- “according to police”
- “according to media reporting”
- “no independent confirmation available”

---

## Interethnic and security incidents

For `TOP-BIH-INTERETHNIC-SECURITY-INCIDENTS`, every incident must include motive discipline:

    MOTIVE ASSESSMENT:
    CONFIRMED ETHNIC MOTIVE / PROBABLE ETHNIC MOTIVE / POSSIBLE ETHNIC MOTIVE / MOTIVE UNCONFIRMED / NON-ETHNIC INCIDENT

Do not classify an incident as interethnic only because the people involved belong to different ethnic groups.

If the motive is unclear, state this clearly.

---

## Disinformation and foreign-linked networks

For `TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`, the monitor must distinguish:

    ATTRIBUTION:
    CONFIRMED COORDINATED CAMPAIGN / STRONGLY INDICATED COORDINATION / POSSIBLE COORDINATION / UNVERIFIED SIGNAL / ORGANIC INFORMATION SPREAD

Do not attribute activity to a foreign state or organization without evidence.

A false claim is not automatically a campaign.

A campaign requires signs of:

- coordination;
- repetition;
- amplification;
- infrastructure;
- funding;
- organized dissemination.

---

## Foreign presence

For `TOP-BIH-FOREIGN-PRESENCE`, every included development must classify the type of presence:

    PRESENCE TYPE:
    DIPLOMATIC / POLITICAL / ECONOMIC / SECURITY / MILITARY / ORGANIZATIONAL / RELIGIOUS / INFORMATIONAL / MIXED

Do not treat all foreign presence as hostile or destabilizing.

Assess whether the presence is:

- routine;
- increased;
- strategically relevant;
- politically sensitive;
- security-relevant;
- linked to domestic actors;
- durable or temporary.

---

## Kosovo spillover

For `TOP-KOSOVO-BIH-SPILLOVER`, every included development must classify the spillover relationship:

    SPILLOVER ASSESSMENT:
    DIRECT SPILLOVER / STRONG INDIRECT EFFECT / POSSIBLE INDIRECT EFFECT / RHETORICAL PARALLEL ONLY / NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER

Do not claim spillover only because Kosovo and BiH are mentioned in the same article.

Look for:

- same actors;
- same networks;
- synchronized messaging;
- copied tactics;
- cross-border mobilization;
- explicit institutional reaction in BiH;
- security measures linked to Kosovo developments.

---

# Cross-topic synthesis

After all topic sections, include:

## Cross-topic assessment

This section must identify whether developments across topics form a broader daily pattern.

Assess whether there is:

- no clear connection;
- weak thematic overlap;
- emerging pattern;
- clear cross-topic escalation;
- insufficient evidence.

Use this format:

    CROSS-TOPIC PATTERN:
    NONE / WEAK / EMERGING / CLEAR / INSUFFICIENT DATA

Then write a short synthesis.

The synthesis must answer:

- Are the developments connected or only coincidental?
- Do they reinforce each other?
- Is there movement from rhetoric to action?
- Is there online-to-offline movement?
- Is there foreign or regional linkage?
- Is there geographic concentration?
- Is there increased severity?

Do not force a pattern.

If there is no evidence of connection, say so.

---

# Unconfirmed signals

Include a separate section only when needed:

## Unconfirmed signals

Use this section for potentially relevant but unverified information.

Unconfirmed signals must not be used as primary evidence for escalation.

For every unconfirmed signal include:

- what is claimed;
- where it appeared;
- why it may matter;
- what confirmation is needed.

Use cautious language.

Do not amplify sensational claims unnecessarily.

---

# Evidence and sources

The briefing must include an evidence section.

Use this format:

    ## Evidence reviewed

    EVENT LOG ITEMS:
    - [EVENT ID]
    - [EVENT ID]

    EXTERNAL SOURCES:
    - [source name / outlet / institution]
    - [source name / outlet / institution]

    UNCONFIRMED OR LOW-CONFIDENCE SOURCES:
    - [source name, if used as signal only]

When the monitor uses `#bih-events-log`, include the EVENT IDs.

When the monitor uses external media or official sources, include:

- source name;
- outlet or institution;
- direct link when available.

Do not cite a source that was not actually reviewed.

Do not invent links.

Do not list a publication merely because another source referenced it.

---

# Overall daily assessment labels

Use the following labels carefully.

## ESCALATION

Use only when evidence shows a clear increase in one or more of the following:

- severity;
- frequency;
- institutional confrontation;
- mobilization;
- security risk;
- cross-border linkage;
- coordinated activity.

A single serious event may justify escalation only when its nature materially changes the risk environment.

Do not use ESCALATION merely because:

- media attention increased;
- rhetoric remained hostile;
- one politician issued a strong statement;
- several articles repeated one event.

---

## LIMITED ESCALATION

Use when one or more meaningful developments indicate increased risk, but evidence is not broad enough for a general escalation judgment.

Examples may include:

- one credible new security incident;
- one meaningful move from rhetoric to action;
- emergence of a new actor;
- limited geographic expansion;
- increased activity without evidence of a broad pattern.

---

## STABLE TENSION

Use when tension remains present but no meaningful new worsening is observed.

This label may be appropriate when:

- underlying disputes remain unresolved;
- hostile rhetoric continues;
- institutions remain under pressure;
- no clear new escalation mechanism appears.

Do not interpret absence of new escalation as resolution.

---

## DE-ESCALATION

Use when evidence shows:

- reduction in tension;
- resolution of disputes;
- lower incident frequency;
- withdrawal of provocative measures;
- credible calming mechanisms;
- restoration of institutional functionality;
- reduced mobilization.

A calm day alone does not prove de-escalation.

---

## MIXED

Use when:

- some topics worsen;
- others stabilize;
- others improve;
- the combined daily picture has no single dominant direction.

Do not use MIXED merely because both positive and negative facts exist.

The label must reflect actual divergent movement.

---

## INSUFFICIENT DATA

Use when:

- the evidence window is too limited;
- developments are too unclear;
- source quality is weak;
- apparent trends cannot be distinguished from noise;
- major claims remain unverified.

Do not force a directional judgment.

---

# Confidence rules

## HIGH

Use only when:

- evidence is official or strongly corroborated;
- developments are clear;
- multiple genuinely independent reliable sources align;
- classification is not speculative;
- factual status is well established.

---

## MEDIUM

Use when:

- evidence is credible but incomplete;
- some uncertainty remains;
- one major source is strong but independent corroboration is limited;
- developments are plausible but need further confirmation;
- interpretation contains limited inference.

---

## LOW

Use when:

- evidence is limited;
- sources are unclear;
- claims are unverified;
- classification depends heavily on interpretation;
- source chains are not independent;
- key factual details remain unresolved.

---

# Significance rules

Use:

- LOW
- MEDIUM
- HIGH
- CRITICAL

## LOW

Use for developments with limited direct effect on the monitored topic.

Examples:

- isolated low-severity event;
- low-impact update;
- limited narrative spread;
- weak organizational relevance.

## MEDIUM

Use for developments that materially affect the topic but do not fundamentally change the risk environment.

Examples:

- credible new incident;
- notable institutional dispute;
- meaningful organizational activity;
- new foreign engagement with limited strategic effect.

## HIGH

Use for developments with substantial:

- security relevance;
- institutional consequence;
- mobilization potential;
- geographic expansion;
- cross-border impact;
- durable strategic effect.

## CRITICAL

Use only for exceptional developments involving:

- immediate major threat to security;
- large-scale violence;
- credible imminent violent mobilization;
- major armed incident;
- severe institutional breakdown with direct security consequences;
- broad coordinated escalation.

Do not assign HIGH or CRITICAL merely because the topic is politically sensitive.

---

# Factual status discipline

The brief must preserve exact factual status.

Do not convert:

`announced intention`
into
`implemented action`

Do not convert:

`proposed`
into
`adopted`

Do not convert:

`adopted`
into
`implemented`

Do not convert:

`investigation opened`
into
`guilt established`

Do not convert:

`reported`
into
`confirmed`

Do not convert:

`possible link`
into
`proven coordination`

Do not convert:

`planned visit`
into
`visit occurred`

Do not convert:

`alleged ethnic motive`
into
`confirmed ethnic motive`

Before publication verify that no factual status has been upgraded without evidence.

---

# Attribution discipline

Always distinguish between:

- official confirmation;
- police statement;
- prosecutorial statement;
- judicial finding;
- political claim;
- media reporting;
- anonymous sourcing;
- social-media signal;
- analytical inference.

When making an inference, signal it clearly.

Preferred formulations in Bulgarian include:

- „по наличните данни“
- „това може да показва“
- „по-вероятно е“
- „наличната информация не позволява категоричен извод“
- „няма независимо потвърждение“
- „твърдението остава непотвърдено“
- „оценката е с ограничена увереност“
- „не може да се изключи“
- „към момента липсват достатъчно данни“

---

# Pattern detection discipline

The brief must distinguish between:

1. isolated development;
2. repeated similar developments;
3. temporal cluster;
4. emerging pattern;
5. confirmed coordinated pattern.

Do not claim a pattern merely because several events occurred close in time.

For an emerging or clear pattern, look for evidence such as:

- same actors;
- same organizations;
- repeated tactics;
- geographic progression;
- coordinated timing;
- shared infrastructure;
- synchronized messaging;
- cross-border links;
- transition from online to offline action;
- increasing severity;
- increasing frequency.

---

# Media amplification discipline

Always distinguish:

- INCIDENT COUNT;
- ARTICLE COUNT;
- VERIFIED DEVELOPMENT COUNT;
- NARRATIVE AMPLIFICATION;
- REAL-WORLD ESCALATION.

Do not convert:

`20 articles about 1 incident`

into:

`20 incidents`

Do not convert:

`viral discussion`

into:

`operational escalation`

However, amplification itself may be relevant under:

`TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`

when evidence supports meaningful coordinated or strategically important spread.

---

# Negative findings discipline

The absence of confirmed evidence must be described carefully.

Distinguish:

`No evidence found`

from:

`Evidence of absence`

It is analytically valid to state:

`Няма съществено ново развитие.`

It is analytically valid to state:

`INSUFFICIENT DATA`

It is analytically valid to state:

`NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER`

Do not interpret lack of detected activity as proof that no activity exists.

---

# Writing style

The daily brief must be:

- concise;
- analytical;
- evidence-based;
- written in Bulgarian;
- calm in tone;
- careful with attribution;
- explicit about uncertainty.

Avoid:

- sensationalism;
- alarmist language;
- unsupported causal claims;
- treating rhetoric as action;
- treating media volume as incident growth;
- treating one event as trend;
- overconfident forecasts;
- unnecessary historical background;
- mechanical article-by-article summaries;
- excessive repetition.

Preferred style:

- short paragraphs;
- clear logic;
- direct explanation of what changed;
- distinction between fact and assessment;
- focus on daily relevance;
- synthesis instead of source-by-source retelling.

The product should read as an analytical monitoring brief, not as a scraped news feed.

---

# Maximum length

Normal daily brief:

- 700–1,200 words.

If no major developments:

- 300–600 words.

If multiple high-significance developments:

- up to 1,500 words.

Do not produce excessively long briefings unless the day clearly requires it.

---

# Final section

Every briefing must end with:

## Watchlist for tomorrow

Include 3–7 concrete things to watch the next day.

Each item must be observable.

Examples:

- дали ще има официална полицейска квалификация на конкретния инцидент;
- дали същият дезинформационен наратив ще бъде повторен от други канали;
- дали ще има реакция от EUFOR, OHR или посолства;
- дали ще се появят нови сходни инциденти в същия район;
- дали политическата реторика ще бъде последвана от институционално действие;
- дали ще се появи независимо потвърждение на непроверен сигнал;
- дали един и същ актьор или мрежа ще се появи в повече от една следена тема.

---

# Final publication check

Before publishing the daily brief verify:

1. The BRIEF ID is correct.
2. The monitoring window is correct.
3. Every registered TOPIC ID was reviewed.
4. No topic was included only because of keyword overlap.
5. Repeated articles were deduplicated.
6. EVENT IDs actually exist when cited.
7. External sources were actually reviewed.
8. Links are real and correspond to reviewed sources.
9. Claims preserve exact factual status.
10. Ethnic motive is not overstated.
11. Foreign attribution is not overstated.
12. Radical or paramilitary labels are evidence-based.
13. Kosovo spillover is demonstrated rather than assumed.
14. Media volume is not confused with incident growth.
15. Cross-topic patterns are not forced.
16. Significance and confidence are not confused.
17. The Executive Summary reflects the actual evidence.
18. The final text is in Bulgarian.
19. The Watchlist contains concrete observable indicators.
20. No source, event, link, or factual detail was fabricated.

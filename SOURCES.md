# SOURCES

## Purpose

This file defines the preferred source framework for the BiH Daily Topic Monitor.

The monitor must use a diversified evidence base.

The purpose is not to maximize the number of sources.

The purpose is to:

- identify meaningful developments;
- detect developments missed by the internal event log;
- verify claims;
- identify contradictions;
- distinguish incidents from media amplification;
- reduce single-source dependence;
- detect local signals;
- improve confidence calibration.

This file defines source priority and handling rules.

It does not imply that every listed source must be checked manually during every run.

The monitor should select sources according to:

- monitored topic;
- development type;
- geographic location;
- actor involved;
- severity;
- need for corroboration.

---

# Core source principle

The monitor must not treat all sources as equal.

For every claim distinguish:

1. SOURCE AUTHENTICITY
Is the source genuinely who it claims to be?

2. DIRECT KNOWLEDGE
Does the source have direct access to the event or decision?

3. FACTUAL RELIABILITY
How strong is the evidence for the factual claim?

4. INTERPRETIVE RELIABILITY
Is the source reporting facts or advancing an interpretation?

5. INDEPENDENCE
Is the apparent corroboration genuinely independent?

6. TIMELINESS
Is the source current enough for the monitored development?

A source may be useful for one purpose and weak for another.

Example:

A political actor is a strong primary source for:

- what that actor said;
- what that actor announced;
- what that actor claims to intend.

The same actor is not automatically a reliable source for:

- whether an opponent committed a crime;
- whether a foreign state controls an organization;
- whether an incident had an ethnic motive;
- whether a security threat is real.

# SOURCE TIER 3

## Established Bosnia and Herzegovina media

The monitor should use a politically and geographically diversified media set.

No single BiH media outlet should define the daily picture.

### General and national coverage

Monitor where relevant:

- Klix.ba;
- N1 BiH;
- BHRT;
- Oslobođenje;
- Dnevni Avaz.

### Republika Srpska and Banja Luka-oriented coverage

Monitor where relevant:

- RTRS;
- BN;
- Nezavisne novine;
- Glas Srpske;
- Capital.ba;
- ATV.

### Investigative and specialist reporting

Monitor where relevant:

- Detektor / BIRN BiH;
- Istraga.ba;
- other credible investigative outlets identified during research.

### Local media

Local media are especially important for:

- interethnic incidents;
- attacks on religious objects;
- protests;
- local security incidents;
- police activity;
- community tensions.

Local reporting must be used carefully.

A local outlet may identify an incident before national media or official institutions.

However:

- motive may be unclear;
- casualty numbers may change;
- attribution may be premature;
- local political framing may distort the event.

Use local media as:

- discovery source;
- early signal;
- location-specific evidence.

Seek confirmation for high-significance claims.

## International media

International media are especially valuable for:

- major security developments;
- international diplomatic shifts;
- foreign presence;
- sanctions;
- cross-border crises;
- independently corroborated high-significance events.

Monitor where relevant:

- Reuters;
- Associated Press;
- BBC;
- Deutsche Welle;

International reporting is not automatically superior to local reporting.

Use it particularly for:

- broader regional context;
- high-level diplomatic developments.

# SOURCE TIER 6

## Disinformation and information-environment sources

For:

`TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`

prioritize specialist evidence.

Monitor where relevant:

- Raskrinkavanje.ba;
- credible regional fact-checking organizations;
- SEE Check-related outputs;
- platform transparency reports;
- Meta transparency material;
- Google transparency material;
- other platform disclosures;
- credible digital-forensics research;
- academic or specialist network analysis;
- investigative reporting on coordinated networks.

A fact-check showing that one claim is false does not automatically prove:

- a coordinated campaign;
- foreign attribution;
- organized amplification;
- state involvement.

Separate:

FALSE CLAIM
from
COORDINATED CAMPAIGN

and separate:

COORDINATED CAMPAIGN
from
FOREIGN-ATTRIBUTED CAMPAIGN

# SOURCE TIER 7

## Specialist security, extremism, and organized-crime sources

Use selectively for:

- radical organizations;
- violent extremism;
- paramilitary activity;
- terrorism;
- organized crime;
- weapons networks;
- foreign-linked security structures.

Possible source types include:

- specialist investigative media;
- established research organizations;
- credible academic centres;
- court-monitoring organizations;
- security-sector reports;
- international organization reports;
- credible extremism-monitoring projects.

Specialist analysis is not equivalent to official confirmation.

Always separate:

- documented fact;
- expert interpretation;
- scenario assessment.

# SOURCE TIER 8

## Social media

Social media may provide:

- early warning;
- primary actor statements;
- visual evidence;
- mobilization signals;
- organizational announcements;
- narrative propagation evidence.

Possible platforms include:

- X;
- Facebook;
- Instagram;
- YouTube;
- TikTok;
- other publicly accessible platforms.

Use social media according to the following rule.

### Primary official account

May be treated as direct evidence of:

- what the actor published;
- what the actor announced;
- what narrative the actor promoted.

It is not automatic proof that factual claims inside the post are true.

### Unofficial account

Treat as:

- signal;
- discovery lead;
- possible amplification evidence.

Require verification before using as established fact.

---

# SOURCE TIER 9

## Telegram and closed or semi-closed networks

Telegram may be highly relevant for:

- radical organizations;
- extremist narratives;
- mobilization;
- cross-border networks;
- Kosovo-related spillover;
- disinformation;
- foreign-linked information activity.

Unless the material is an authenticated primary statement, treat Telegram content as:

- SIGNAL;
- UNCONFIRMED CLAIM;
- NETWORK ACTIVITY EVIDENCE;
- NARRATIVE EVIDENCE.

Do not automatically treat it as evidence that a real-world event occurred.

Example:

A Telegram channel calling for mobilization is evidence that:

- the call was published.

It is not automatically evidence that:

- mobilization occurred.

# Corroboration rules

## Rule 1 — One article is not multiple sources

If five outlets copy the same agency report:

CORROBORATION COUNT:
1 underlying source chain

Do not treat repetition as independent confirmation.

---

## Rule 2 — Primary source plus media repetition

Example:

A politician posts a claim.

Ten outlets reproduce it.

This confirms:

- the politician made the claim.

It does not confirm:

- the claim is factually true.

---

## Rule 3 — Sensitive security claims

For claims involving:

- paramilitary groups;
- weapons;
- terrorism;
- foreign intelligence activity;
- planned violence;
- ethnic motive;
- foreign state coordination;

seek stronger corroboration.

Prefer:

- official confirmation;
- direct documentary evidence;
- multiple genuinely independent credible sources.

If unavailable, preserve uncertainty.

---

## Rule 4 — Visual evidence

Images and videos must be checked for:

- original date;
- original location;
- earlier publication;
- misleading caption;
- editing;
- whether the material actually shows the claimed event.

Do not treat a video as current only because it was posted today.

---

## Rule 5 — Anonymous sourcing

Anonymous claims may be used only with caution.

Assess:

- outlet reliability;
- specificity;
- whether later confirmation exists;
- whether the source has direct knowledge.

Do not use anonymous claims as sole basis for:

- CRITICAL significance;
- foreign attribution;
- paramilitary classification;
- confirmed ethnic motive.

---

# Media amplification discipline

The monitor must distinguish:

1. EVENT FREQUENCY
How many distinct developments occurred?

2. ARTICLE FREQUENCY
How many articles were published?

3. NARRATIVE AMPLIFICATION
How widely was a claim repeated?

4. REAL-WORLD ESCALATION
Did behavior or conditions actually change?

Do not convert:

20 articles about 1 incident
→ 20 incidents

Do not convert:

viral discussion
→ operational escalation

However, media amplification itself may be relevant under:

`TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`

when evidence shows meaningful coordinated or strategically significant spread.

---

# Geographic discovery rule

For local incidents, search using:

- municipality name;
- settlement name;
- canton;
- police jurisdiction;
- entity;
- nearby symbolic or religious location.

Do not rely only on national-level searches.

Important local incidents may appear first in small local outlets.

---

# Language search rule

Search in relevant languages.

Where technically possible use:

- Bosnian;
- Croatian;
- Serbian Latin;
- Serbian Cyrillic;
- English.

For Kosovo spillover also consider:

- Albanian-language searches when technically available.

Do not assume English-language search alone provides adequate coverage.

---

# Search expansion rule

For every monitored topic, search both:

1. DIRECT TERMS
and
2. CONCEPTUAL INDICATORS

Example:

Do not search only:

`paravojna grupa`

Also consider conceptually relevant terms such as:

- obuka;
- kamp;
- naoružana grupa;
- uniformisana grupa;
- regrutovanje;
- veteranska mreža;
- ekstremistička organizacija;
- oružje;
- mobilizacija.

Keyword appearance alone does not establish relevance.

---

# Source disagreement

When reliable sources disagree:

1. identify the disagreement;
2. do not silently choose one version;
3. determine whether the disagreement concerns:
   - basic fact;
   - number;
   - motive;
   - attribution;
   - interpretation;
4. preserve uncertainty;
5. explain what confirmation is needed.

Preferred wording in Bulgarian:

- „Източниците се разминават относно...“
- „Няма независимо потвърждение на...“
- „Официалната версия се оспорва от...“
- „Наличните данни не позволяват категоричен извод.“

---

# Dynamic source discovery

The monitor may use a relevant source not listed in this file.

When doing so:

- assess the source;
- identify its role;
- seek corroboration where necessary;
- do not automatically treat it as trusted.

Do not automatically edit this registry.

Permanent source additions must be deliberate.

---

# Daily source diversity rule

For active topics, avoid relying entirely on one political, geographic, or media environment.

Where relevant, seek evidence across:

- Sarajevo-based sources;
- RS-based sources;
- local sources;
- official institutions;
- international sources.

This does not require artificial balance.

A false claim does not become credible because two political sides are quoted.

The goal is evidence diversity, not mechanical symmetry.

---

# Final source audit

Before publishing a Daily Topic Brief verify:

1. Were external sources actually reviewed?
2. Were repeated articles deduplicated?
3. Is apparent corroboration genuinely independent?
4. Were official claims distinguished from verified facts?
5. Were local incidents checked against competent authorities where possible?
6. Was ethnic motive preserved at the correct confidence level?
7. Was foreign attribution supported?
8. Was social-media content treated at the correct evidence level?
9. Was media volume separated from real-world activity?
10. Were source disagreements disclosed?
11. Were links included when available?
12. Were no sources fabricated?

The final brief must never claim a source was reviewed when it was not actually accessed.

# EARLY WARNING INDICATORS — RADICAL AND PARAMILITARY ACTIVITY

## Linked topic

`TOP-BIH-RADICAL-PARAMILITARY-ACTIVITY`

## Topic name

Активност на паравоенни, радикални, екстремистки и насилствено ориентирани организации и мрежи в Босна и Херцеговина

---

# Purpose

This file defines the early-warning and escalation-indicator framework for:

`TOP-BIH-RADICAL-PARAMILITARY-ACTIVITY`

Its purpose is to help the BiH Daily Topic Monitor distinguish between:

- inflammatory rhetoric;
- symbolic radical activity;
- organizational development;
- recruitment;
- training;
- logistical preparation;
- armed capability;
- operational preparation;
- imminent violent risk.

The monitor must not label an actor, organization, association, group, network, club, veterans' structure, ultras group, motorcycle organization, religious network, political movement, or informal gathering as:

- paramilitary;
- extremist;
- terrorist;
- violent;
- armed;

without sufficient evidence.

The framework must reduce both:

- false positives;
- missed escalation signals.

---

# Exact monitored variable

The monitored variable is:

**Change in the organization, capability, coordination, recruitment, training, logistics, armament, cross-border connectivity, mobilization potential, and operational activity of actors or networks with plausible relevance to politically, ethnically, ideologically, religiously, or otherwise motivated violence in BiH.**

The monitor is not measuring:

- controversial opinion;
- radical rhetoric alone;
- legal political activism;
- ordinary veterans' activity;
- lawful firearm ownership;
- ordinary sports-fan activity;
- symbolic nationalism alone;
- foreign nationality;
- association with a politically controversial actor alone.

The central question is:

**Is there observable movement from expression or identity toward organized capability, mobilization, coercion, preparation, or violence?**

---

# Core analytical distinction

Always distinguish:

1. RHETORIC
2. SYMBOLIC ACTIVITY
3. ORGANIZATIONAL ACTIVITY
4. RECRUITMENT
5. TRAINING
6. LOGISTICAL PREPARATION
7. WEAPONS OR ARMED CAPABILITY
8. OPERATIONAL PREPARATION
9. EXECUTED VIOLENT ACTION
10. OFFICIAL INVESTIGATION OR DISRUPTION

These are not interchangeable.

A radical statement does not prove:

`ORGANIZATIONAL ACTIVITY`

An organized gathering does not prove:

`PARAMILITARY TRAINING`

Military-style clothing does not prove:

`ARMED FORMATION`

A weapons seizure does not prove:

`POLITICAL OR EXTREMIST NETWORK`

A police investigation does not prove:

`GUILT`

---

# Indicator interpretation rule

Indicators are classified as:

- LOW-LEVEL
- MEDIUM-LEVEL
- HIGH-LEVEL
- CRITICAL

This classification reflects warning value.

It does not automatically determine:

- DAILY ASSESSMENT;
- SIGNIFICANCE;
- OVERALL DAILY ASSESSMENT.

Always assess:

- evidence quality;
- actor capability;
- continuity;
- organizational structure;
- access to weapons;
- cross-border links;
- intent;
- target selection;
- operational security;
- official response.

---

# LOW-LEVEL INDICATORS

Low-level indicators may suggest emerging radicalization or organizational interest but do not demonstrate violent capability.

Examples include:

- increased inflammatory rhetoric by a known radical actor;
- new glorification of political violence;
- glorification of convicted war criminals linked to current mobilization messaging;
- renewed circulation of violent historical narratives;
- explicit enemy construction;
- dehumanizing rhetoric;
- calls for confrontation without operational detail;
- increased dissemination of extremist symbols;
- creation of new online channels;
- sudden growth of radical online communities;
- increased propaganda production;
- new public appearances by previously inactive radical actors;
- symbolic uniformed appearances;
- repeated gatherings of ideologically aligned individuals;
- foreign ideological contacts;
- public praise of foreign armed groups;
- dissemination of manuals without evidence of use;
- expressions of interest in training;
- fundraising appeals with unclear purpose;
- isolated recruitment language;
- increased security-sensitive rhetoric around anniversaries or crises.

## Interpretation

LOW-level indicators may justify:

- expanded monitoring;
- actor-specific searches;
- network mapping;
- watchlist inclusion;
- source verification.

They must not independently justify:

`PARAMILITARY ACTIVITY`

or:

`ESCALATION`

---

# MEDIUM-LEVEL INDICATORS

Medium-level indicators show movement from expression toward organization or capability.

Examples include:

- creation of a new structured organization;
- visible leadership hierarchy;
- recurring closed gatherings;
- systematic recruitment efforts;
- screening or selection of members;
- collection of membership data for mobilization purposes;
- organized transport to sensitive events;
- coordinated deployment of members to several locations;
- repeated military-style physical training;
- tactical training claims with partial corroboration;
- organized field exercises;
- use of remote training locations;
- acquisition of uniforms or standardized equipment at scale;
- fundraising for security-related equipment;
- acquisition of radios or communications equipment;
- structured encrypted communication groups;
- new links with foreign radical organizations;
- recurring cross-border meetings;
- movement of organizers between BiH and neighboring states;
- documented participation of the same network in confrontational events;
- intimidation of opponents by organized groups;
- credible allegations of weapons access requiring further verification;
- official police interest;
- preliminary investigation by competent authorities;
- seizure of suspicious equipment without confirmed violent intent;
- organized presence at politically sensitive events with coercive potential.

## Interpretation

A verified MEDIUM-level indicator may support:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

when:

- it represents real organizational growth;
- capability increases;
- recruitment expands;
- cross-border links strengthen;
- the actor moves closer to coercive or violent capacity.

Several mutually reinforcing MEDIUM indicators may constitute an emerging high-risk pattern.

---

# HIGH-LEVEL INDICATORS

High-level indicators demonstrate substantial capability or preparation.

Examples include:

- verified weapons acquisition by an organized network;
- illegal stockpiling of weapons;
- explosives linked to a political, ethnic, ideological, or extremist network;
- repeated tactical or military-style training;
- verified training camps;
- firearms training outside ordinary lawful context;
- structured command and control;
- operational cells;
- compartmentalized communication;
- surveillance of potential targets;
- target lists;
- reconnaissance;
- acquisition of protective equipment for confrontation;
- covert logistics;
- transport of weapons;
- cross-border movement of trained members;
- direct links to foreign violent or paramilitary networks;
- foreign funding tied to operational capability;
- recruitment for violent action;
- credible threats backed by organizational capability;
- coordination with politically relevant actors for coercive purposes;
- deployment of organized groups to intimidate or control public space;
- repeated use of violence by members of the same network;
- official counterterrorism or major organized-security operation;
- indictments involving violent extremist preparation;
- evidence of transition from online radicalization to offline preparation.

## Interpretation

HIGH-level indicators may justify:

`ESCALATING`

when:

- verified;
- materially new;
- operationally relevant;
- linked to a clear increase in capability or intent.

Do not assign HIGH because of political sensitivity alone.

---

# CRITICAL INDICATORS

Critical indicators represent imminent or executed severe risk.

Examples include:

- credible imminent plan for organized violence;
- confirmed deployment of armed members;
- coordinated armed mobilization;
- operational orders for attack;
- active preparation of explosives for a target;
- movement of weapons immediately before planned action;
- established target selection combined with capability;
- mass-casualty planning;
- direct armed confrontation;
- coordinated attacks;
- seizure of territory or facilities by an armed group;
- attacks on police, military, institutions, religious sites, or communities by an organized network;
- cross-border infiltration of armed actors;
- rapid transfer of trained personnel for imminent action;
- credible command structure directing violence;
- confirmed foreign operational support for imminent violent activity;
- emergency security action explicitly linked to an imminent organized threat.

## Interpretation

CRITICAL indicators require:

- very strong verification;
- urgent distinction between allegation and confirmed fact;
- active contradiction search;
- source-chain audit.

CRITICAL must remain exceptional.

---

# Activity ladder

The monitor should assess where activity sits on the following ladder:

RHETORIC
→ SYMBOLIC ACTIVITY
→ ORGANIZATION
→ RECRUITMENT
→ TRAINING
→ LOGISTICS
→ WEAPONS
→ OPERATIONAL PREPARATION
→ VIOLENT ACTION

Movement upward on this ladder is analytically more important than the absolute level of rhetoric.

Example:

known extremist rhetoric
→ new structured recruitment
→ increased organizational capability

This may represent escalation.

Example:

hostile rhetoric
→ more hostile rhetoric

This may remain stable.

---

# Escalation mechanisms

The monitor must identify the exact mechanism.

Use:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

---

## Mechanism 1 — Rhetoric to organization

Diffuse radical rhetoric
→ structured group formation
→ increased capacity for coordinated action

---

## Mechanism 2 — Organization to recruitment

Existing network
→ active recruitment campaign
→ larger mobilization pool

---

## Mechanism 3 — Recruitment to training

New members
→ repeated tactical or weapons-related training
→ increased operational capability

---

## Mechanism 4 — Training to weapons access

Training activity
→ verified weapons acquisition
→ materially higher violence potential

---

## Mechanism 5 — Domestic network to cross-border support

Local organization
→ recurring foreign organizational links
→ increased resources, expertise, or resilience

---

## Mechanism 6 — Online network to offline mobilization

Digital propaganda
→ coordinated physical gathering
→ increased real-world confrontation potential

---

## Mechanism 7 — Symbolic presence to coercive deployment

Uniformed symbolic appearance
→ organized deployment to sensitive locations
→ increased intimidation or confrontation risk

---

## Mechanism 8 — Network activity to operational preparation

Organizational activity
→ target surveillance, logistics, or operational planning
→ immediate increase in threat

If no clear mechanism exists:

do not force escalation.

# Primary evidence requirement

Assessment must be driven mainly by:

`PRIMARY ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE`

Examples:

- verified recruitment;
- documented organization;
- verified training;
- weapons seizure with organizational link;
- command structure;
- operational planning;
- police or prosecutorial evidence;
- court documentation;
- authenticated primary organizational material;
- verified cross-border movement.

The following normally remain:

`SUPPORTING CONTEXT`

unless linked to operational change:

- political rhetoric;
- historical associations;
- ideological sympathy;
- symbolic insignia;
- foreign travel;
- media speculation;
- unnamed intelligence claims;
- photographs without verified date and location.

---

# Organizational evidence discipline

Do not infer an organization from:

- several people appearing together;
- common clothing;
- common political views;
- common nationality;
- common ethnicity.

Look for:

- name;
- leadership;
- membership;
- recurring structure;
- communications;
- financing;
- logistics;
- task allocation;
- repeated activity.

---

# Paramilitary classification discipline

Use the term:

`PARAMILITARY`

only when evidence supports characteristics such as:

- organized structure;
- military-style hierarchy;
- training;
- coercive capability;
- weapons access;
- operational role outside ordinary legal security structures.

Do not use paramilitary merely because:

- uniforms appear;
- members are veterans;
- military symbols are displayed;
- actors use nationalist rhetoric.

When evidence is incomplete use formulations such as:

- „структура с предполагаеми паравоенни характеристики“
- „група, за която се твърди, че провежда военизирана подготовка“
- „няма независимо потвърждение за паравоенен характер“

---

# Extremist classification discipline

Do not label an actor as:

`EXTREMIST`

solely because views are:

- nationalist;
- conservative;
- religious;
- anti-Western;
- anti-government;
- separatist.

Look for evidence of:

- endorsement of violence;
- coercive exclusion;
- dehumanization linked to action;
- organized violent intent;
- participation in violent networks.

---

# Terrorism classification discipline

Use:

`TERRORISM`

only when supported by:

- competent official classification;
- relevant legal proceedings;
- strong factual evidence consistent with the legal context.

Do not use terrorism as a rhetorical synonym for:

- radical;
- violent;
- armed;
- criminal.

---

# Weapons evidence discipline

A weapons-related development must distinguish:

- lawful weapon;
- unlawful weapon;
- individual possession;
- organizational stockpile;
- historical weapon;
- operational weapon;
- ammunition;
- explosive device;
- dual-use equipment.

Do not infer network capability from one individual's unrelated firearm possession.

Ask:

- Who controls the weapon?
- Is there an organizational link?
- What quantity?
- What type?
- What context?
- Is intent known?

---

# Training evidence discipline

Distinguish:

- fitness training;
- sports training;
- airsoft;
- hunting;
- legal shooting practice;
- civil-protection training;
- military-style training;
- tactical training;
- weapons training;
- operational rehearsal.

Do not call an activity:

`PARAMILITARY TRAINING`

without evidence about:

- content;
- participants;
- purpose;
- location;
- organization.

---

# Camp evidence discipline

The term:

`TRAINING CAMP`

must not be used merely because a group gathered in a remote location.

Look for:

- recurring site use;
- structured curriculum;
- instructors;
- tactical activity;
- weapons;
- organizational control;
- participant recruitment;
- verified testimony;
- police or judicial evidence.

Unconfirmed claims about camps should remain:

`UNCONFIRMED SIGNAL`

until stronger evidence appears.

---

# Foreign-link discipline

A foreign link may include:

- organizational relationship;
- funding;
- training;
- instructors;
- equipment;
- command influence;
- logistical support;
- recurring coordination.

Do not infer foreign control from:

- nationality;
- travel;
- ideological sympathy;
- one photograph;
- one meeting.

Classify carefully:

- CONTACT
- RECURRING CONTACT
- ORGANIZATIONAL LINK
- MATERIAL SUPPORT
- OPERATIONAL SUPPORT
- CONTROL

These levels are not interchangeable.

---

# Official investigation discipline

An investigation indicates:

- official concern;
- potential evidentiary basis;
- need for monitoring.

It does not prove:

- guilt;
- extremist nature;
- paramilitary status;
- planned violence.

Preserve exact status:

PRELIMINARY CHECK
INVESTIGATION
ARREST
CHARGE
INDICTMENT
TRIAL
CONVICTION

Do not upgrade one into another.

# De-escalation indicators

Possible de-escalation indicators include:

- dissolution of a relevant group;
- recruitment decline;
- cancellation of planned mobilization;
- abandonment of training activity;
- surrender or seizure of weapons;
- disruption of logistics;
- arrest of operational leadership;
- reduction in cross-border support;
- public distancing followed by organizational change;
- closure of communication channels combined with verified inactivity;
- successful prevention action by authorities;
- demobilization;
- reduction in repeated activity.

A temporary quiet period alone does not prove de-escalation.

---

# Stabilization indicators

Possible stabilization indicators include:

- radical rhetoric continues without organizational growth;
- no follow-on recruitment;
- no training evidence;
- no weapons linkage;
- no geographic expansion;
- no offline mobilization;
- suspected activity remains unconfirmed;
- competent authorities find no immediate threat;
- planned gathering remains peaceful.

These may support:

`STABLE`

or:

`INSUFFICIENT DATA`

# Evidence requirements

For LOW-level indicators:

- credible discovery source may be sufficient;
- corroboration preferred.

For MEDIUM-level indicators:

- seek primary organizational material;
- official source;
- or independent corroboration.

For HIGH-level indicators:

- seek police, prosecution, court, documentary, or multiple genuinely independent sources;
- perform contradiction search.

For CRITICAL indicators:

- use the highest available verification standard;
- actively search for:
  - denial;
  - location error;
  - date error;
  - old imagery;
  - source-chain dependence;
  - manipulated content.

# Daily assessment decision rules

## STABLE

Use when:

- radical rhetoric exists;
- no verified growth in organization or capability appears;
- no movement upward on the activity ladder is demonstrated.

---

## LIMITED ESCALATION

Use when:

- at least one verified development increases organizational or mobilization capability;
- movement from rhetoric to organization, recruitment, training, or logistics is visible;
- scope remains limited.

Before assigning, state internally:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

---

## ESCALATING

Use when:

- capability materially increases;
- several mutually reinforcing indicators appear;
- training, weapons, cross-border support, or operational preparation strengthens;
- geographic or organizational scope expands.

---

## MIXED

Use only when:

- one capability dimension worsens;
- another genuinely weakens.

Do not use MIXED merely because confirmed and unconfirmed information coexist.

---

## INSUFFICIENT DATA

Use when:

- activity depends on one unverified claim;
- organizational structure is unclear;
- source chains are weak;
- current activity cannot be distinguished from historical reputation.

---

# Significance discipline

Do not confuse:

`SIGNIFICANCE`

with:

`CONFIDENCE`

or:

`ESCALATION`

Example:

A claim about an armed camp may be:

HIGH potential significance
+
LOW confidence

It must not automatically produce:

ESCALATING

Example:

A verified small recruitment effort may be:

MEDIUM significance
+
LIMITED ESCALATION

---

# Watchlist requirements

Watch indicators must be:

- specific;
- observable;
- feasible;
- linked to capability change.

Good examples:

- whether police open a formal investigation;
- whether alleged training receives independent confirmation;
- whether named organizers recur at additional gatherings;
- whether weapons are linked to a specific organization;
- whether cross-border contacts become recurring;
- whether online recruitment results in physical meetings;
- whether a claimed camp is independently geolocated;
- whether prosecutors file charges.

Bad examples:

- “monitor extremists”;
- “watch radicalization”;
- “see whether groups become more active”.

---

# Final topic audit

Before assigning the daily assessment for:

`TOP-BIH-RADICAL-PARAMILITARY-ACTIVITY`

verify:

1. What exactly changed today?
2. What is the previous comparison condition?
3. Where is the activity on the ladder:
   RHETORIC → ORGANIZATION → RECRUITMENT → TRAINING → LOGISTICS → WEAPONS → OPERATIONAL PREPARATION → VIOLENCE?
4. Which items are PRIMARY ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE?
5. Which items are SUPPORTING CONTEXT?
6. Which items are UNCONFIRMED SIGNALS?
7. Is organizational structure demonstrated?
8. Is capability demonstrated?
9. Is intent demonstrated?
10. Are weapons actually linked to the organization?
11. Is training correctly classified?
12. Are foreign links demonstrated rather than inferred?
13. Are historical allegations being mistaken for current activity?
14. Are media labels being repeated without evidence?
15. Is the escalation mechanism explicit?
16. Would the assessment remain justified without unconfirmed claims?
17. Are source chains genuinely independent?
18. Are watchlist items concrete and feasible?

If the evidence does not demonstrate organizational or capability change:

prefer:

`STABLE`

or:

`INSUFFICIENT DATA`

rather than forcing:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

# EARLY WARNING INDICATORS — FOREIGN PRESENCE AND EXTERNAL ACTIVITY

## Linked topic

`TOP-BIH-FOREIGN-PRESENCE`

## Topic name

Динамика на чуждестранното присъствие и външната активност в Босна и Херцеговина с потенциално значение за политическата стабилност, сигурността, институционалното функциониране и стратегическото влияние

---

# Purpose

This file defines the early-warning and escalation-indicator framework for:

`TOP-BIH-FOREIGN-PRESENCE`

Its purpose is to help the BiH Daily Topic Monitor distinguish between:

- routine diplomatic activity;
- normal economic engagement;
- ordinary international cooperation;
- public diplomacy;
- political influence;
- strategic investment;
- security cooperation;
- organizational penetration;
- sustained external leverage;
- covert or opaque influence;
- operational foreign involvement;
- foreign activity with direct security consequences.

The monitor must avoid both:

- treating every foreign visit, investment, organization, citizen, embassy action, or public statement as malign influence;
- missing material changes in external capability, access, leverage, coordination, or operational presence.

The central analytical principle is:

**Foreign presence is not inherently destabilizing, and foreign activity is not automatically foreign interference.**

---

# Exact monitored variable

The monitored variable is:

**Change in the scale, type, persistence, access, institutional penetration, material capability, organizational footprint, political leverage, security relevance, military relevance, informational reach, economic dependence, and operational effect of external actors in Bosnia and Herzegovina.**

The monitor is not measuring:

- all diplomacy;
- all foreign visits;
- all foreign investment;
- all international organizations;
- all embassy statements;
- all foreign citizens;
- all cultural activity;
- all religious links;
- all business contacts;
- all military cooperation;
- all international aid.

The central question is:

**Has an external actor materially changed its presence, access, leverage, capability, persistence, or operational effect in BiH compared with the previous known condition?**

- RELIGIOUS

- ordinary foreign investment;

# LOW-LEVEL INDICATORS

Low-level indicators suggest possible change in external interest, access, or activity but do not demonstrate material expansion.

Examples include:

- first public meeting between relevant actors;
- unusual diplomatic messaging;
- increased frequency of public statements;
- exploratory investment discussions;
- announced visit by senior foreign official;
- new cooperation memorandum without implementation;
- new cultural or organizational initiative;
- creation of local representative office;
- new foreign-funded media or civic initiative;
- increased public-diplomacy activity;
- new religious or ideological contact;
- isolated security-cooperation discussion;
- announcement of possible equipment donation;
- new political endorsement;
- increased foreign media attention;
- foreign delegation visiting a sensitive institution;
- new but unverified claim of external support;
- isolated appearance of foreign-linked organization;
- increased narrative visibility of an external actor.

## Interpretation

LOW-level indicators may justify:

- expanded monitoring;
- actor mapping;
- implementation checks;
- financial or organizational follow-up;
- watchlist inclusion.

They must not independently justify:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

or:

`INCREASED FOREIGN CONTROL`

---

# MEDIUM-LEVEL INDICATORS

Medium-level indicators demonstrate meaningful growth in presence, access, persistence, leverage, or capability.

Examples include:

- signed agreement with concrete implementation provisions;
- recurring high-level political coordination;
- new permanent office or organizational structure;
- new long-term foreign-funded program;
- recurring foreign financing to politically relevant actor or network;
- significant strategic investment in sensitive sector;
- acquisition of key infrastructure by foreign-linked entity;
- expanded security cooperation;
- repeated foreign training of local personnel;
- delivery of security-relevant equipment;
- recurring access to strategic institutions;
- new permanent advisory role;
- sustained foreign organizational footprint;
- recurring coordination with political actors;
- significant increase in foreign military or security activity;
- expansion of an existing mission;
- new foreign-linked information infrastructure;
- documented foreign support to a domestic advocacy or mobilization network;
- material cross-border organizational integration;
- opaque financing with strategic relevance;
- recurring foreign-linked activity around elections or institutional crises.

## Interpretation

A verified MEDIUM-level indicator may support:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

only when:

- the change is materially new;
- access, leverage, capability, or operational effect increases;
- the changed risk mechanism is explicit.

Several mutually reinforcing MEDIUM indicators may indicate strategic expansion.

---

# HIGH-LEVEL INDICATORS

High-level indicators demonstrate substantial external capability, leverage, penetration, or operational relevance.

Examples include:

- documented foreign operational support to politically relevant network;
- sustained foreign financing of coercive or destabilizing activity;
- foreign training linked to non-state security capability;
- significant covert or deceptive organizational activity;
- foreign-directed influence network;
- direct external support to institutional non-compliance;
- material foreign backing for parallel structures;
- foreign provision of security or military capability outside normal transparent frameworks;
- repeated deployment of foreign personnel with unclear legal basis;
- verified intelligence-related operational activity;
- strategic control over critical infrastructure producing political leverage;
- severe economic dependence converted into explicit political pressure;
- documented foreign coordination of domestic destabilizing actors;
- external support to radical or paramilitary network;
- coordinated multi-domain foreign activity across political, informational, economic, and security sectors;
- material expansion of military posture linked to deterioration;
- operational cross-border support to coercive actors;
- foreign influence activity producing measurable institutional consequences.

## Interpretation

HIGH-level indicators may justify:

`ESCALATING`

when:

- verified;
- materially new;
- consequential;
- linked to clear increased leverage, capability, or operational effect.

Do not assign HIGH because an actor is geopolitically controversial.

---

# CRITICAL INDICATORS

Critical indicators represent acute foreign-enabled or foreign-driven security risk.

Examples include:

- confirmed foreign operational support for imminent violence;
- deployment of armed foreign personnel outside lawful framework;
- coordinated external support to armed mobilization;
- direct foreign control of violent network;
- covert transfer of weapons for imminent action;
- foreign intelligence operation directly enabling violent attack;
- externally supported seizure or coercive control of institutions;
- major cross-border movement of armed actors;
- foreign military action affecting BiH territory;
- foreign-supported multi-location destabilization;
- direct external command of organized violent activity;
- emergency security response to imminent foreign-enabled threat.

## Interpretation

CRITICAL requires:

- strongest available verification;
- active contradiction search;
- precise distinction between allegation and confirmed fact.

CRITICAL must remain exceptional.

# Foreign presence ladder

Assess activity on the following ladder:

CONTACT
→ RECURRING ENGAGEMENT
→ AGREEMENT
→ IMPLEMENTATION
→ ORGANIZATIONAL FOOTPRINT
→ MATERIAL SUPPORT
→ DEPENDENCE
→ LEVERAGE
→ OPERATIONAL SUPPORT
→ DIRECTION
→ CONTROL

Movement upward on this ladder is more important than raw activity volume.

Example:

more diplomatic meetings
→ no change in access or implementation

may remain stable.

Example:

political contact
→ recurring financing
→ organizational dependence

may represent significant change.

# Escalation mechanisms

The monitor must identify the exact mechanism.

Use:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

---

## Mechanism 1 — Contact to recurring coordination

Isolated meeting
→ repeated structured engagement
→ increased access and influence potential

---

## Mechanism 2 — Agreement to implementation

Political memorandum
→ funded and operationalized program
→ increased material footprint

---

## Mechanism 3 — Investment to strategic leverage

Ordinary economic presence
→ control of strategically important asset
→ increased political or economic leverage

---

## Mechanism 4 — Support to dependence

Recurring external funding
→ domestic actor becomes materially dependent
→ increased external leverage

---

## Mechanism 5 — Public diplomacy to network infrastructure

Messaging activity
→ creation of durable media or organizational network
→ increased influence persistence

---

## Mechanism 6 — Security cooperation to operational capability

Training or equipment cooperation
→ sustained operational capability
→ increased security relevance

---

## Mechanism 7 — Political alignment to coordinated action

Shared positions
→ repeated synchronized institutional behavior
→ increased external influence over domestic process

This does not automatically prove foreign direction.

---

## Mechanism 8 — Foreign organizational link to material support

Informal contacts
→ funding, logistics, training, or equipment
→ increased domestic actor capability

---

## Mechanism 9 — External actor to cross-topic reinforcement

Foreign presence
→ support to information, radical, political, or mobilization network
→ increased persistence and convergence of risks

---

## Mechanism 10 — Presence to operational involvement

Routine access
→ direct participation in implementation or coercive activity
→ materially higher security relevance

If no clear mechanism exists:

do not force escalation.

# Diplomatic activity discipline

Routine diplomacy must not be overstated.

Ask:

- Is the actor new?
- Is access new?
- Is frequency materially higher?
- Is policy changing?
- Is an agreement implemented?
- Is there an operational consequence?

More meetings do not automatically mean more influence.

- influence;

# Proxy discipline

Do not use:

`PROXY`

# Influence vs interference discipline

Distinguish:

`INFLUENCE`
=
attempt to shape preferences or outcomes

from:

`INTERFERENCE`
=
covert, deceptive, coercive, unlawful, or otherwise improper intervention in a domestic process

Do not use:

`FOREIGN INTERFERENCE`

without evidence of the relevant conduct.

---

# Leverage discipline

Foreign leverage may derive from:

- financing;
- debt;
- ownership;
- energy dependence;
- security support;
- political protection;
- institutional access;
- organizational dependence.

Leverage is not the same as control.

Ask:

- Can the external actor impose costs?
- Can it condition support?
- Can it alter domestic behavior?
- Is there evidence this leverage is actually used?

# De-escalation indicators

Possible de-escalation indicators include:

- withdrawal of personnel;
- closure of organizational office;
- suspension of funding;
- cancellation of project;
- reduced military posture;
- termination of strategic agreement;
- loss of institutional access;
- reduction in recurring coordination;
- divestment from strategic asset;
- disruption of operational support;
- increased transparency reducing uncertainty;
- restoration of domestic autonomy after dependency;
- international mission returning from exceptional to routine posture.

A quiet media period alone does not prove de-escalation.

---

# Stabilization indicators

Possible stabilization indicators include:

- foreign activity remains within established baseline;
- announced initiative is not implemented;
- no new permanent footprint appears;
- no expansion of access;
- no operational consequence;
- routine diplomatic activity continues;
- temporary visit ends as scheduled;
- security cooperation remains within existing framework;
- suspected foreign link remains unconfirmed.

These may support:

`STABLE`

# Evidence requirements

For LOW-level indicators:

- credible discovery source may be sufficient;
- implementation follow-up preferred.

For MEDIUM-level indicators:

- seek official documents;
- financial records;
- ownership records;
- agreements;
- primary actor statements;
- credible independent corroboration.

For HIGH-level indicators:

- seek strong documentary, official, financial, organizational, security, or judicial evidence;
- perform contradiction search.

For CRITICAL indicators:

- use highest available verification standard;
- actively test:
  - actor identity;
  - legal status;
  - presence scale;
  - location;
  - mandate;
  - funding;
  - operational link;
  - source dependence.

Prioritize where relevant:

1. Official agreements and government records
2. Company and ownership records
3. Financial documentation
4. Security and defense institutions
5. International missions
6. Embassies and foreign ministries
7. Court and prosecutorial records
8. Primary organizational records

# Discovery vs confirmation discipline

A source may be useful for:

`DISCOVERY`

but insufficient for:

`CONFIRMATION`

Example:

Media allegation of foreign-funded network
→ discovery signal

Financial record or documented organizational relationship
→ stronger confirmation

Do not confuse these roles.

# Daily assessment decision rules

## STABLE

Use when:

- foreign activity exists;
- no meaningful increase in access, leverage, footprint, capability, or operational effect appears;
- activity remains within established baseline.

---

## LIMITED ESCALATION

Use when:

- at least one verified development materially increases foreign access, leverage, organizational footprint, capability, or security relevance;
- a clear escalation mechanism exists;
- scope remains limited.

Before assigning, state internally:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

Example:

sporadic political contact
→ recurring structured coordination plus material support
→ increased external leverage over domestic actor

---

## ESCALATING

Use when:

- several primary indicators worsen;
- foreign footprint expands;
- leverage increases;
- support becomes operational;
- multi-domain activity converges;
- geographic or institutional reach grows.

---

## MIXED

Use only when:

- one dimension of foreign presence increases;
- another genuinely decreases.

Do not use MIXED merely because several different foreign actors pursue competing interests.

---

## INSUFFICIENT DATA

Use when:

- foreign linkage depends on nationality or ideology;
- implementation is unclear;
- funding is opaque but unverified;
- source chains are weak;
- activity cannot be distinguished from normal diplomacy or commerce.

---

# Significance discipline

Do not confuse:

`SIGNIFICANCE`

with:

`ESCALATION`

or:

`CONFIDENCE`

Example:

A major foreign investment may be:

HIGH significance
+
STABLE daily assessment

Example:

A small but verified new operational support arrangement may be:

MEDIUM significance
+
LIMITED ESCALATION

# Watchlist requirements

Watch indicators must be:

- specific;
- observable;
- procedurally feasible;
- linked to a defined mechanism.

Good examples:

- whether a signed agreement receives funding;
- whether a foreign office becomes operational;
- whether announced security equipment is delivered;
- whether a temporary deployment is extended;
- whether recurring political meetings produce coordinated institutional action;
- whether ownership of a strategic asset changes;
- whether alleged foreign funding receives documentary confirmation;
- whether a foreign-linked organization opens additional local branches;
- whether a competent authority confirms security-related foreign activity.

Bad examples:

- “monitor foreign influence”;

`TOP-BIH-FOREIGN-PRESENCE`

1. What exactly changed today?
2. What is the previous comparison condition?
3. What type of foreign presence is involved?
4. Is the actor state, private, organizational, religious, military, security, or mixed?

6. Is implementation demonstrated?
7. Is access increasing?
8. Is organizational footprint increasing?
9. Is material support increasing?

11. Is operational effect demonstrated?

14. Are alignment and control being confused?

# EARLY WARNING INDICATORS — KOSOVO TO BIH SPILLOVER

## Linked topic

`TOP-KOSOVO-BIH-SPILLOVER`

## Topic name

Прехвърляне на напрежение, наративи, мобилизация, актьори, организационни практики и рискове от динамиката около Косово към Босна и Херцеговина

---

# Purpose

This file defines the early-warning and escalation-indicator framework for:

`TOP-KOSOVO-BIH-SPILLOVER`

Its purpose is to help the BiH Daily Topic Monitor distinguish between:

- ordinary political commentary about Kosovo;
- rhetorical analogy;
- symbolic solidarity;
- narrative transfer;
- political exploitation of Kosovo-related developments;
- organizational linkage;
- actor overlap;
- mobilization transfer;
- tactical imitation;
- cross-border reinforcement;
- direct spillover with security consequences.

The monitor must avoid both:

- treating every mention of Kosovo in BiH as spillover;
- missing genuine transfer mechanisms because developments appear separately across Kosovo, Serbia, Republika Srpska, the Federation of BiH, Brčko District, or wider regional networks.

The central analytical principle is:

**Temporal proximity and rhetorical similarity do not prove spillover. A transfer mechanism must be demonstrated.**

---

# Exact monitored variable

The monitored variable is:

**Change in the degree to which developments connected to Kosovo materially affect political behavior, institutional action, narratives, mobilization, organizational networks, security posture, radical activity, interethnic incidents, or broader stability in Bosnia and Herzegovina.**

The monitor is not measuring:

- all Kosovo news;
- all Serbia–Kosovo disputes;
- all statements by BiH politicians about Kosovo;
- all references to Republika Srpska and Kosovo;
- all regional nationalist rhetoric;
- all diplomatic positions on Kosovo recognition;
- all Serbian political activity;
- all Albanian political activity;
- all regional media attention.

The central question is:

**Has a Kosovo-related development produced an observable effect, transfer, activation, adaptation, or reinforcement inside BiH?**

---

# Required spillover classification

For every meaningful suspected spillover development classify:

- DIRECT
- STRONG INDIRECT
- POSSIBLE INDIRECT
- RHETORICAL PARALLEL ONLY
- NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER

Do not skip classification when a development is included under this topic.

---

# DIRECT

Use only when a clear transfer mechanism from Kosovo-related dynamics to BiH is demonstrated.

Possible evidence includes:

- same actors operating across both environments;
- direct organizational coordination;
- verified movement of personnel;
- verified movement of weapons or equipment;
- direct financing;
- documented operational support;
- explicit mobilization in BiH triggered by a Kosovo event;
- direct institutional action in BiH caused by a Kosovo-related development;
- verified security-posture change in BiH explicitly linked to Kosovo;
- direct copy of a tactic combined with actor or organizational linkage;
- official evidence of cross-border operational connection.

DIRECT must remain a high evidentiary threshold.

---

# STRONG INDIRECT

Use when the causal link is not fully direct but several mutually reinforcing indicators show that Kosovo-related dynamics materially affect BiH.

Possible indicators include:

- explicit adoption of Kosovo-related mobilization narratives by capable BiH actors;
- repeated activation of the same political or organizational network;
- documented coordination through shared intermediaries;
- recurring transfer of tactics or messaging;
- Kosovo-related event produces measurable institutional reaction in BiH;
- significant mobilization around an imported Kosovo narrative;
- cross-border network amplifies and operationalizes the issue in BiH;
- same external actor reinforces both environments in a linked manner.

STRONG INDIRECT requires more than commentary.

---

# POSSIBLE INDIRECT

Use when a Kosovo-related influence mechanism is plausible but incomplete.

Examples include:

- rapid adoption of the same narrative by domestic actors;
- symbolic mobilization after a Kosovo-related event;
- suspicious overlap in actors;
- repeated cross-border amplification;
- similar tactics appearing shortly afterward;
- emerging organizational contacts;
- political use of Kosovo analogy tied to a domestic dispute;
- increased activity by actors with known regional links.

Possible indirect spillover must not be presented as established.

---

# RHETORICAL PARALLEL ONLY

Use when:

- actors compare Kosovo and BiH;
- politicians invoke precedent;
- similar language appears;
- media discuss analogy;
- symbolic solidarity is expressed;
- no material transfer mechanism is demonstrated.

Examples:

- “If Kosovo can do X, Republika Srpska can do Y”;
- claims about double standards;
- comparison between Kosovo independence and RS status;
- references to territorial precedent;
- general solidarity statements.

Rhetorical parallel may be politically significant.

But it is not by itself spillover.

---

# NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER

Use when:

- Kosovo-related development is externally important;
- BiH actors comment on it;
- no measurable effect inside BiH is identified;
- no actor, organizational, institutional, mobilization, or security transfer is demonstrated.

This is a valid and important negative finding.

---

# Core analytical distinction

Always distinguish:

1. KOSOVO EVENT
2. BIH REACTION
3. RHETORICAL PARALLEL
4. NARRATIVE TRANSFER
5. POLITICAL ADOPTION
6. ORGANIZATIONAL LINKAGE
7. ACTOR OVERLAP
8. MOBILIZATION TRANSFER
9. TACTICAL TRANSFER
10. MATERIAL SUPPORT
11. SECURITY EFFECT
12. OPERATIONAL SPILLOVER

These are not interchangeable.

A reaction does not prove:

`SPILLOVER`

Narrative similarity does not prove:

`COORDINATION`

Same geopolitical alignment does not prove:

`COMMON CONTROL`

A copied tactic does not prove:

`ACTOR LINKAGE`

---

# Baseline / normal condition

The following must not automatically be treated as Kosovo-to-BiH spillover:

- BiH politicians commenting on Kosovo;
- RS politicians comparing RS and Kosovo;
- Sarajevo-based politicians criticizing Serbia over Kosovo;
- media coverage of Kosovo tensions;
- diplomatic statements;
- discussion of recognition;
- references to Dayton;
- references to territorial integrity;
- references to self-determination;
- Serbian nationalist rhetoric;
- Albanian nationalist rhetoric;
- social-media solidarity;
- flag displays;
- historical comparisons;
- election messaging;
- ordinary Serbia–RS political contacts;
- regional media amplification;
- public concern about regional instability;
- routine KFOR activity;
- routine EU or NATO commentary.

These may become relevant only when additional evidence demonstrates transfer into BiH.

# Indicator interpretation rule

Indicators are classified as:

- LOW-LEVEL
- MEDIUM-LEVEL
- HIGH-LEVEL
- CRITICAL

This classification reflects warning value.

It does not automatically determine:

- DAILY ASSESSMENT;
- SIGNIFICANCE;
- OVERALL DAILY ASSESSMENT.

Always assess:

- causal linkage;
- timing;
- actor overlap;
- organizational connection;
- capability;
- mobilization;
- implementation;
- geographic spread;
- security effect;
- source confidence.

---

# LOW-LEVEL INDICATORS

Low-level indicators suggest possible transfer or increased sensitivity but do not demonstrate meaningful spillover.

Examples include:

- sudden increase in Kosovo analogies by major BiH actors;
- new use of Kosovo precedent in domestic political dispute;
- increased claims of regional war risk;
- symbolic solidarity events;
- increased flag or identity symbolism;
- rapid domestic amplification of a Kosovo-related narrative;
- new calls for political response in BiH;
- increased hostile framing toward another community;
- coordinated-looking but unverified hashtag activity;
- new media narrative linking Kosovo and RS;
- public calls for institutional debate;
- isolated protest announcement;
- increased rhetoric about self-determination;
- new claims that a Kosovo event changes BiH constitutional rights;
- unusual attention by domestic radical actors;
- isolated references to security mobilization;
- unverified claims of cross-border activist coordination.

## Interpretation

LOW-level indicators may justify:

- expanded monitoring;
- actor-specific search;
- cross-border network search;
- watchlist inclusion.

They must not independently justify:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

or:

`DIRECT SPILLOVER`

---

# MEDIUM-LEVEL INDICATORS

Medium-level indicators demonstrate meaningful movement from commentary toward transfer, activation, or domestic consequence.

Examples include:

- organized protest in BiH explicitly triggered by a Kosovo-related event;
- repeated mobilization by the same actors after Kosovo developments;
- formal institutional initiative in BiH explicitly tied to Kosovo;
- coordinated political action using Kosovo as an operational precedent;
- recurring cross-border organizational contacts;
- shared organizers between Kosovo/Serbia-related activity and BiH mobilization;
- movement of activists between relevant locations;
- recurring dissemination by the same cross-border network;
- adoption of specific tactics previously used in Kosovo-related confrontation;
- increased police posture in BiH explicitly linked to regional Kosovo tension;
- official security assessment of spillover risk;
- mobilization of radical or ultras networks around Kosovo events;
- organized transport to Kosovo/Serbia-related demonstrations;
- fundraising linked to Kosovo-related confrontation with domestic organizational effects;
- coordinated symbolic actions across multiple BiH locations;
- domestic institutional obstruction explicitly activated by Kosovo precedent;
- credible evidence of foreign or regional actors using Kosovo-related dynamics to mobilize BiH actors.

## Interpretation

A verified MEDIUM-level indicator may support:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

when:

- a real transfer mechanism is demonstrated;
- the domestic effect is materially new;
- scope remains limited;
- evidence is sufficiently verified.

Several mutually reinforcing MEDIUM indicators may indicate an emerging spillover pattern.

---

# HIGH-LEVEL INDICATORS

High-level indicators demonstrate substantial transfer with political, organizational, security, or operational consequences.

Examples include:

- verified movement of organized actors connected to Kosovo-related confrontation into BiH activity;
- direct cross-border operational coordination;
- documented transfer of equipment or logistics;
- verified foreign or regional support to domestic mobilization;
- same radical network active in both Kosovo-related and BiH contexts;
- coordinated multi-location mobilization in BiH triggered by Kosovo events;
- serious interethnic incidents in BiH directly linked to Kosovo-related mobilization;
- organized retaliatory activity;
- security institutions activate exceptional measures due to spillover risk;
- verified tactical transfer combined with actor overlap;
- documented cross-border command or coordination;
- direct use of Kosovo-related crisis to activate coercive domestic structures;
- movement of weapons connected to the regional tension;
- synchronized information, political, and physical mobilization;
- clear convergence between Kosovo spillover and radical or paramilitary activity;
- significant regional crisis causes measurable deterioration of BiH security environment.

## Interpretation

HIGH-level indicators may justify:

`ESCALATING`

when:

- verified;
- materially new;
- consequential;
- linked to a clear transfer mechanism.

Do not assign HIGH merely because Kosovo itself is experiencing a serious crisis.

The question is the effect inside BiH.

---

# CRITICAL INDICATORS

Critical indicators represent acute cross-border or regional transfer of severe security risk.

Examples include:

- confirmed movement of armed actors into BiH linked to Kosovo-related confrontation;
- transfer of weapons for imminent domestic use;
- coordinated regional violent mobilization affecting BiH;
- direct operational support to armed groups in BiH;
- simultaneous linked violent incidents across regional locations;
- immediate retaliatory mobilization in BiH after Kosovo-related violence;
- cross-border command of violent action;
- major interethnic violence in BiH directly triggered by Kosovo-related developments;
- emergency security measures responding to imminent spillover;
- organized attacks using transferred actors, logistics, or operational planning;
- regional crisis causing direct breakdown of local security control in BiH.

## Interpretation

CRITICAL requires:

- strongest available verification;
- active contradiction search;
- precise causal linkage.

CRITICAL must remain exceptional.

---

# Spillover ladder

Assess developments on the following ladder:

COMMENTARY
→ RHETORICAL PARALLEL
→ NARRATIVE TRANSFER
→ POLITICAL ADOPTION
→ ORGANIZATIONAL LINKAGE
→ ACTOR OVERLAP
→ MOBILIZATION TRANSFER
→ TACTICAL TRANSFER
→ MATERIAL SUPPORT
→ SECURITY CONSEQUENCE
→ OPERATIONAL SPILLOVER

Movement upward on this ladder is more important than raw mention volume.

Example:

more references to Kosovo
→ more references to Kosovo

may remain stable.

Example:

Kosovo-related crisis
→ domestic organizational mobilization
→ multi-location physical action

may represent escalation.

# Escalation mechanisms

The monitor must identify the exact mechanism.

Use:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

---

## Mechanism 1 — Commentary to political adoption

External Kosovo event
→ major BiH actor adopts it as basis for concrete domestic action
→ increased institutional confrontation risk

---

## Mechanism 2 — Narrative transfer to mobilization

Kosovo-related narrative
→ organized domestic mobilization call
→ increased real-world confrontation potential

---

## Mechanism 3 — Symbolic solidarity to organized action

Public solidarity
→ recurring structured gatherings
→ increased organizational capability

---

## Mechanism 4 — Actor overlap

Separate regional and domestic activity
→ same organizers or network identified
→ increased probability of coordinated transfer

---

## Mechanism 5 — Organizational linkage to material support

Cross-border contacts
→ financing, logistics, transport, equipment, or training
→ increased domestic capability

---

## Mechanism 6 — Tactical imitation

Known Kosovo-related tactic
→ repeated adoption in BiH by linked actors
→ increased confrontation capability

Tactical similarity alone is insufficient.

---

## Mechanism 7 — Regional crisis to security posture change

Kosovo deterioration
→ exceptional BiH security measures
→ demonstrated institutional assessment of spillover risk

---

## Mechanism 8 — Information transfer to offline action

Kosovo-related false or escalatory narrative
→ domestic mobilization or incident
→ increased security risk

---

## Mechanism 9 — Political precedent to institutional confrontation

Kosovo precedent invoked rhetorically
→ concrete constitutional or institutional action in BiH
→ increased confrontation risk

---

## Mechanism 10 — Local incident to regional reinforcement

Domestic incident
→ Kosovo/Serbia-linked actors amplify or support it
→ increased persistence and mobilization potential

---

## Mechanism 11 — Regional network convergence

Separate actors
→ common intermediary, funding, organizer, or infrastructure
→ increased cross-border operational resilience

---

## Mechanism 12 — Crisis transfer to inter-community retaliation

Kosovo-related violence
→ inflammatory domestic framing
→ retaliatory threats or incidents in BiH

If no clear mechanism exists:

do not force spillover assessment.

# Primary evidence requirement

Assessment must be driven mainly by:

`PRIMARY ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE`

Examples:

- official institutional action;
- verified mobilization;
- documented actor overlap;
- organizational records;
- direct communications;
- movement records where lawfully available;
- financial evidence;
- logistics evidence;
- security-posture change;
- police or prosecutorial evidence;
- authenticated primary material;
- documented cross-border coordination;
- verified domestic consequence.

The following normally remain:

`SUPPORTING CONTEXT`

unless they materially change risk:

- political commentary;
- historical analogy;
- media speculation;
- geopolitical interpretation;
- anniversary context;
- ideological similarity;
- increased article volume;
- generic warnings about regional instability.

---

# Causal linkage discipline

Before assigning spillover ask:

1. What happened in Kosovo or the Kosovo-related regional environment?
2. What changed in BiH afterward?
3. What connects the two?
4. Is the connection direct, indirect, possible, rhetorical, or absent?
5. Is there a plausible alternative explanation?

Do not rely only on:

`A happened before B`

Temporal sequence alone does not prove causation.

---

# Counterfactual test

Ask internally:

**Would the BiH development probably have occurred even without the Kosovo-related event?**

If yes:

the spillover claim may be weak.

Example:

A long-planned RS political initiative
+
same-day Kosovo tension

does not automatically mean spillover.

---

# Alternative explanation discipline

For every significant suspected spillover test alternatives such as:

- domestic election dynamics;
- existing constitutional dispute;
- anniversary effect;
- unrelated local incident;
- routine Serbia–RS coordination;
- media amplification;
- opportunistic political rhetoric;
- pre-existing organizational activity.

Do not choose spillover merely because it is geopolitically attractive.

---

# Actor overlap discipline

Actor overlap may be important.

But distinguish:

- same individual;
- same organization;
- same umbrella network;
- same funder;
- same intermediary;
- same ideological milieu.

These are not equivalent.

One actor appearing in two contexts does not automatically prove organizational transfer.

Assess:

- role;
- frequency;
- timing;
- coordination;
- capability.

---

# Organizational linkage discipline

Look for:

- recurring meetings;
- shared leadership;
- common funding;
- common logistics;
- common communications;
- shared event organization;
- repeated personnel movement;
- joint statements followed by action;
- documented task division.

Do not infer organization from symbolic solidarity.

---

# Serbia linkage discipline

Serbia may be a relevant actor in Kosovo-related dynamics and BiH.

But distinguish:

- Serbian state institution;
- Serbian political party;
- RS–Serbia institutional relationship;
- private organization;
- nationalist network;
- media outlet;
- informal actor.

Do not collapse them into:

`SERBIA`

Do not infer state direction merely from Serbian origin.

---

# Kosovo actor discipline

Distinguish:

- Kosovo Government;
- Kosovo Police;
- political parties;
- municipal actors;
- KFOR;
- EULEX;
- civil society;
- Albanian nationalist groups;
- Serbian community structures;
- informal networks.

Do not treat all Kosovo-related actors as one system.

---

# KFOR / EULEX discipline

Routine KFOR or EULEX activity does not by itself demonstrate spillover into BiH.

Relevant questions include:

- Did posture materially change?
- Did BiH institutions respond?
- Did regional actors activate linked networks?
- Was a specific security mechanism transferred?

---

# Narrative transfer discipline

For each suspected transferred narrative identify:

- ORIGINAL CLAIM
- ORIGINAL CONTEXT
- DOMESTIC ADOPTER
- DOMESTIC AUDIENCE
- MODIFICATION
- AMPLIFICATION
- MOBILIZATION EFFECT
- OFFLINE CONSEQUENCE

A copied phrase alone does not prove meaningful spillover.

---

# Tactical transfer discipline

A tactic may include:

- roadblocks;
- coordinated protests;
- municipal obstruction;
- barricades;
- symbolic occupation;
- coordinated flag actions;
- rapid Telegram mobilization;
- decentralized protest cells;
- targeted institutional disruption.

But similar tactics may arise independently.

Require additional evidence such as:

- actor overlap;
- direct communication;
- explicit imitation;
- shared organizers;
- timing and sequence;
- recurring pattern.

---

# Mobilization discipline

Distinguish:

- rhetorical call;
- online support;
- sign-up activity;
- transport organization;
- physical gathering;
- repeated deployment;
- coordinated multi-location action.

Do not describe online expressions of support as physical mobilization.

---

# Geographic discipline

Identify where the domestic effect occurs:

- STATE LEVEL
- RS
- FBIH
- BRČKO
- CANTON
- MUNICIPALITY
- MULTI-LOCATION

One local reaction does not prove national spillover.

---

# Temporal discipline

Assess:

- immediate reaction;
- delayed reaction;
- recurring response;
- sustained transfer.

Very short timing may support relevance.

But timing alone is insufficient.

---

# Information-operation discipline

Kosovo-related narratives may interact with disinformation.

Distinguish:

- accurate reporting;
- false claim;
- manipulated claim;
- coordinated amplification;
- foreign-linked campaign.

Do not call all regional propaganda:

`SPILLOVER`

unless a domestic effect in BiH is demonstrated.

---

# Security posture discipline

A BiH security response may be strong evidence when:

- competent authority explicitly cites Kosovo-related risk;
- deployment changes;
- protective measures change;
- border posture changes;
- intelligence coordination increases;
- public-event protection changes.

Routine police activity must not be overstated.

---

# Border discipline

For cross-border concerns assess:

- legal crossing;
- irregular crossing;
- organized personnel movement;
- equipment movement;
- weapons movement;
- logistics;
- repeated route use.

Do not infer security spillover from ordinary travel.

---

# Weapons discipline

A weapons-related spillover claim requires evidence connecting:

- weapon;
- actor;
- route;
- organization;
- purpose.

Do not infer Kosovo linkage merely because a weapon originates from the region.

---

# Political precedent discipline

Kosovo is often invoked as precedent.

Distinguish:

- rhetorical analogy;
- legal argument;
- policy proposal;
- institutional action;
- implementation.

Only movement toward concrete domestic effect increases spillover relevance.

---

# Source-chain discipline

Do not treat:

multiple articles

as:

multiple independent confirmations

Ask:

- Are outlets repeating one Serbian source?
- Are they repeating one Kosovo source?
- Are they copying an agency?
- Are they all citing one politician?
- Is the same anonymous claim circulating regionally?

When independence is unclear use:

`reported by several outlets`

rather than:

`independently confirmed`

# De-escalation indicators

Possible de-escalation indicators include:

- Kosovo-related mobilization call is withdrawn;
- planned domestic protest is cancelled;
- no follow-on domestic action occurs;
- security posture returns to baseline;
- cross-border activity declines;
- political actors stop operationalizing Kosovo precedent;
- alleged network linkage is disproven;
- mobilized groups demobilize;
- no retaliation follows a sensitive event;
- institutional communication reduces uncertainty;
- regional tension declines and domestic activation subsides;
- linked information campaign loses traction before offline effect.

A decline in Kosovo media coverage alone does not prove de-escalation.

---

# Stabilization indicators

Possible stabilization indicators include:

- domestic reaction remains rhetorical;
- no organizational linkage appears;
- no actor movement is demonstrated;
- no mobilization occurs;
- no security-posture change occurs;
- no interethnic incident follows;
- no geographic spread appears;
- domestic institutions continue normal functioning;
- suspected spillover remains unconfirmed;
- Kosovo references decline without real-world effect.

These may support:

`STABLE`

or:

`NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER`

# Evidence requirements

For LOW-level indicators:

- credible discovery source may be sufficient;
- causal linkage remains unproven.

For MEDIUM-level indicators:

- seek primary statements;
- organizational evidence;
- official records;
- actor overlap;
- independent corroboration.

For HIGH-level indicators:

- seek strong official, documentary, financial, organizational, security, or judicial evidence;
- perform contradiction search;
- test alternative explanations.

For CRITICAL indicators:

- use highest available verification standard;
- actively test:
  - actor identity;
  - timing;
  - route;
  - location;
  - organizational link;
  - weapons link;
  - causal chain;
  - source dependence.

---

# Source hierarchy for this topic

Prioritize where relevant:

1. Competent BiH security institutions
2. Kosovo Police
3. KFOR
4. EULEX
5. Serbian competent institutions
6. BiH police and prosecutors
7. Official government and institutional records
8. EU and US official sources
9. Primary organizational material
10. Credible investigative journalism
11. Established BiH media
12. Established Kosovo and Serbian media
13. Regional media
14. Local media
15. Social media and Telegram as signal layer

Do not use hierarchy mechanically.

Source role matters.

---

# Geographic source diversity

For significant suspected spillover seek evidence from more than one information environment where feasible:

- BiH;
- Kosovo;
- Serbia;
- international institutional sources.

This helps identify:

- source-chain dependence;
- competing narratives;
- missing context;
- false attribution.

Do not require artificial symmetry when one side has the primary competent source.

# Language-search discipline

Where relevant search in:

- Bosnian;
- Croatian;
- Serbian Latin;
- Serbian Cyrillic;
- English;
- Albanian.

Language diversity improves discovery.

It does not automatically improve source independence.

# Discovery vs confirmation discipline

A source may be useful for:

`DISCOVERY`

but insufficient for:

`CONFIRMATION`

Example:

Telegram claim of activists moving from Serbia toward BiH
→ discovery signal

Official record, verified imagery, direct reporting, or multiple independent sources
→ possible confirmation

Do not confuse these roles.

# Daily assessment decision rules

## STABLE

Use when:

- Kosovo-related developments exist;
- BiH reactions remain within normal rhetorical or political baseline;
- no meaningful transfer mechanism appears.

---

## LIMITED ESCALATION

Use when:

- at least one verified Kosovo-related development produces a materially new effect inside BiH;
- a clear transfer mechanism is demonstrated;
- scope remains limited.

Before assigning, state internally:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ KOSOVO-RELATED DEVELOPMENT
→ DOMESTIC TRANSFER MECHANISM
→ CHANGED RISK

Example:

routine rhetorical references
→ acute Kosovo event
→ organized domestic mobilization by recurring network
→ increased confrontation risk

---

## ESCALATING

Use when:

- several primary indicators demonstrate transfer;
- actor overlap increases;
- mobilization expands;
- organization strengthens;
- geographic spread appears;
- security consequences emerge.

---

## MIXED

Use only when:

- one spillover dimension worsens;
- another genuinely weakens.

Do not use MIXED merely because Kosovo and BiH developments move differently.

---

## INSUFFICIENT DATA

Use when:

- causal linkage is unclear;
- timing is the main evidence;
- actor overlap is speculative;
- source chains are weak;
- domestic development may have an independent cause.

---

# Significance discipline

Do not confuse:

`SIGNIFICANCE`

with:

`SPILLOVER`

or:

`ESCALATION`

Example:

Major violence in Kosovo may be:

HIGH regional significance
+
NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER to BiH

Example:

A small but verified domestic mobilization linked directly to Kosovo may be:

MEDIUM significance
+
LIMITED ESCALATION

---

# Negative finding discipline

A strong daily finding may be:

`NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER`

even during a severe Kosovo crisis.

This is analytically valuable.

Do not manufacture domestic linkage merely because the external event is important.

---

# Watchlist requirements

Watch indicators must be:

- specific;
- observable;
- temporally plausible;
- linked to a defined transfer mechanism.

Good examples:

- whether a Kosovo-related mobilization call produces a physical gathering in BiH;
- whether the same organizers appear in both regional and BiH activity;
- whether a competent BiH authority changes security posture explicitly due to Kosovo developments;
- whether a Kosovo-related narrative migrates into coordinated domestic mobilization;
- whether cross-border transport of activists is independently confirmed;
- whether RS institutions convert Kosovo precedent rhetoric into a concrete institutional measure;
- whether a regional radical network activates a BiH branch;
- whether a Kosovo-linked incident triggers retaliatory threats or incidents in BiH;
- whether alleged shared funding receives documentary confirmation.

Bad examples:

- “monitor Kosovo”;
- “watch regional tensions”;
- “see whether spillover occurs”;
- “check Serbia and Kosovo”.

---

# Final topic audit

Before assigning the daily assessment for:

`TOP-KOSOVO-BIH-SPILLOVER`

verify:

1. What exactly happened in the Kosovo-related environment?
2. What exactly changed inside BiH?
3. What connects the two?
4. Is the link DIRECT, STRONG INDIRECT, POSSIBLE INDIRECT, RHETORICAL PARALLEL ONLY, or NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER?
5. Is timing being mistaken for causation?
6. Is rhetorical similarity being mistaken for coordination?
7. Is domestic political opportunism a better explanation?
8. Would the BiH development likely have occurred anyway?
9. Is actor overlap demonstrated?
10. Is organizational linkage demonstrated?
11. Is mobilization demonstrated?
12. Is tactical transfer demonstrated?
13. Is material support demonstrated?
14. Is a security-posture change demonstrated?
15. Is geographic spread demonstrated?
16. Are Serbia-related and Kosovo-related dynamics being improperly collapsed?
17. Are different actors being treated as one system?
18. Which items are PRIMARY ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE?
19. Which items are SUPPORTING CONTEXT?
20. Which items are UNCONFIRMED SIGNALS?
21. Is the exact transfer mechanism explicit?
22. Would the assessment remain justified if speculative linkages were removed?
23. Are alternative explanations tested?
24. Are source chains genuinely independent?
25. Are watchlist items concrete and feasible?

If evidence does not demonstrate an actual transfer mechanism into BiH:

prefer:

`STABLE`

`INSUFFICIENT DATA`

or:

`NO DEMONSTRATED SPILLOVER`

rather than forcing:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

# EARLY WARNING INDICATORS — DISINFORMATION AND FOREIGN-LINKED NETWORKS

## Linked topic

`TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`

## Topic name

Дезинформационни кампании и дейност на чуждестранно или външно свързани информационни мрежи, способни да влияят върху сигурността, политическата стабилност и общественото напрежение в Босна и Херцеговина

---

# Purpose

This file defines the early-warning and escalation-indicator framework for:

`TOP-BIH-DISINFORMATION-FOREIGN-NETWORKS`

Its purpose is to help the BiH Daily Topic Monitor distinguish between:

- false information;
- inaccurate reporting;
- political propaganda;
- partisan messaging;
- organic narrative convergence;
- coordinated amplification;
- organized influence activity;
- foreign-linked information activity;
- coordinated disinformation campaign;
- information activity connected to offline mobilization or security risk.

The monitor must avoid both:

- treating every false claim as a coordinated campaign;
- missing systematic information operations because individual items appear separately.

The central analytical principle is:

**False content alone does not prove coordination, and coordination alone does not prove foreign direction.**

---

# Exact monitored variable

The monitored variable is:

**Change in the scale, coordination, persistence, cross-platform spread, actor connectivity, foreign linkage, target selection, narrative synchronization, behavioral effect, and security relevance of deceptive or manipulative information activity affecting BiH.**

The monitor is not measuring:

- all political propaganda;
- all biased journalism;
- all false statements;
- all rumors;
- all partisan narratives;
- all foreign media coverage;
- all social-media controversy;
- all criticism of Western, Russian, Serbian, Croatian, Turkish, Gulf, EU, NATO, OHR, or BiH institutions.

The central question is:

**Is there observable movement from isolated information activity toward coordinated, persistent, externally reinforced, behaviorally consequential, or security-relevant influence activity?**

---

# Core analytical distinction

Always distinguish:

1. FALSE OR MISLEADING CLAIM
2. ORGANIC AMPLIFICATION
3. PARTISAN AMPLIFICATION
4. COORDINATED AMPLIFICATION
5. NETWORKED DISSEMINATION
6. FOREIGN LINKAGE
7. FOREIGN SUPPORT
8. FOREIGN DIRECTION
9. BEHAVIORAL EFFECT
10. OFFLINE MOBILIZATION
11. SECURITY CONSEQUENCE

These are not interchangeable.

A false claim does not prove:

`COORDINATED CAMPAIGN`

Several accounts repeating the same claim do not prove:

`FOREIGN NETWORK`

A foreign-language source does not prove:

`FOREIGN DIRECTION`

A pro-Russian narrative does not prove:

`RUSSIAN OPERATION`

A pro-Western narrative does not prove:

`WESTERN OPERATION`

A viral post does not prove:

`BEHAVIORAL EFFECT`

---

# Required attribution classification

For every significant suspected campaign or network classify:

- CONFIRMED COORDINATED CAMPAIGN
- STRONGLY INDICATED
- POSSIBLE
- UNVERIFIED SIGNAL
- ORGANIC / NON-COORDINATED

Do not skip attribution classification when the development is included under this topic.

---

# CONFIRMED COORDINATED CAMPAIGN

Use only when strong evidence demonstrates deliberate coordination.

Possible evidence includes:

- platform takedown attribution;
- official investigation;
- court or prosecutorial evidence;
- authenticated internal coordination material;
- documented common infrastructure;
- verified centrally managed account network;
- coordinated publication records;
- technical forensic evidence;
- direct financial or organizational linkage;
- credible investigation demonstrating common control.

CONFIRMED must remain exceptional.

---

# STRONGLY INDICATED

Use when several mutually reinforcing indicators strongly suggest coordination but definitive proof remains incomplete.

Possible indicators include:

- synchronized posting;
- repeated identical or near-identical text;
- common URL infrastructure;
- coordinated account creation;
- common administrative behavior;
- repeated synchronized amplification;
- shared media assets;
- highly consistent timing;
- repeated cross-platform sequencing;
- common organizers;
- independently documented foreign organizational links.

STRONGLY INDICATED requires more than narrative similarity.

---

# POSSIBLE

Use when coordination or foreign linkage is plausible but evidence remains incomplete.

Examples:

- unusual synchronization;
- repeated identical framing;
- recurring account cluster;
- unexplained cross-border amplification;
- common narrative appearing rapidly across linked ecosystems;
- suspicious but unverified infrastructure overlap.

Possible must not be presented as established attribution.

---

# UNVERIFIED SIGNAL

Use when:

- claim may be important;
- evidence is weak;
- source reliability is uncertain;
- attribution is speculative;
- technical confirmation is absent.

UNVERIFIED SIGNAL may justify:

- expanded search;
- preservation of evidence;
- actor mapping;
- watchlist inclusion.

It must not independently determine:

- ESCALATION;
- HIGH significance;
- foreign attribution;
- coordinated-campaign classification.

---

# ORGANIC / NON-COORDINATED

Use when available evidence indicates:

- independent repetition;
- normal partisan convergence;
- ordinary news diffusion;
- spontaneous public reaction;
- multiple actors responding to same event;
- no demonstrated coordination.

Narrative similarity alone may be organic.

---

# Baseline / normal condition

The following must not automatically be treated as coordinated disinformation or foreign influence:

- politician makes false statement;
- media outlet publishes inaccurate information;
- partisan outlets use similar framing;
- several accounts repeat major news;
- pro-Russian commentary;
- pro-EU commentary;
- pro-Serbian commentary;
- pro-Croatian commentary;
- pro-Bosniak commentary;
- anti-OHR rhetoric;
- anti-NATO rhetoric;
- criticism of sanctions;
- criticism of Western policy;
- criticism of Russia;
- criticism of Serbia;
- foreign media reporting about BiH;
- embassy public diplomacy;
- ordinary diplomatic messaging;
- viral rumor;
- satirical content;
- translation of foreign article;
- ideological similarity.

Additional evidence is required before assigning coordination or foreign direction.

# Indicator interpretation rule

Indicators are classified as:

- LOW-LEVEL
- MEDIUM-LEVEL
- HIGH-LEVEL
- CRITICAL

This classification reflects warning value.

It does not automatically determine:

- DAILY ASSESSMENT;
- SIGNIFICANCE;
- OVERALL DAILY ASSESSMENT.

Always assess:

- falsity or manipulation;
- coordination;
- persistence;
- audience reach;
- actor structure;
- foreign linkage;
- target selection;
- behavioral effect;
- offline consequence;
- source confidence.

---

# LOW-LEVEL INDICATORS

Low-level indicators suggest possible narrative manipulation or emerging amplification but do not establish a coordinated campaign.

Examples include:

- new false or misleading claim about a sensitive issue;
- rapid spread of an unverified rumor;
- repeated emotionally manipulative framing;
- sudden increase in hostile narrative volume;
- new fabricated quote;
- misleading image;
- old image presented as current;
- selective editing;
- false institutional claim;
- false security warning;
- exaggerated casualty claim;
- false claim about troop movement;
- false claim about arrest or violence;
- new conspiracy narrative;
- coordinated-looking hashtag without proof of coordination;
- isolated foreign-media amplification;
- suspicious anonymous account activity;
- new Telegram channel focused on sensitive BiH themes;
- rapid reposting across ideologically aligned outlets;
- repeated claims of imminent conflict without evidence;
- false claims around elections, memorials, religious events, or security incidents.

## Interpretation

LOW-level indicators may justify:

- verification search;
- original-source tracing;
- account-cluster review;
- watchlist inclusion.

They must not independently justify:

`COORDINATED CAMPAIGN`

or:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

---

# MEDIUM-LEVEL INDICATORS

Medium-level indicators show movement toward systematic amplification, network behavior, or meaningful influence activity.

Examples include:

- repeated synchronized posting across multiple accounts;
- identical or near-identical text across several outlets;
- recurring account cluster amplifying same narratives;
- rapid cross-platform dissemination;
- repeated use of common media assets;
- coordinated hashtag activation;
- systematic amplification by connected pages;
- recurring foreign and domestic actor interaction;
- repeated narrative introduction by one source and amplification by a stable network;
- cross-border amplification involving same actors;
- new fake media brand or impersonation account;
- coordinated harassment of journalists, institutions, or communities;
- repeated false security narratives;
- fabricated documents;
- manipulated official-looking notices;
- organized amplification around sensitive anniversaries;
- systematic targeting of trust in security institutions;
- coordinated claims of imminent conflict;
- network activity designed to intensify ethnic fear;
- repeated disinformation linked to protest mobilization;
- evidence of financial or organizational support requiring further verification;
- official fact-checking or institutional warning about a recurring campaign.

## Interpretation

A verified MEDIUM-level indicator may support:

`LIMITED ESCALATION`

when:

- coordination materially increases;
- persistence grows;
- a new capable network appears;
- security-relevant narratives intensify;
- behavioral consequences become plausible.

Several mutually reinforcing MEDIUM indicators may constitute an emerging campaign.

---

# HIGH-LEVEL INDICATORS

High-level indicators demonstrate substantial coordination, foreign support, behavioral effect, or security relevance.

Examples include:

- verified coordinated account network;
- documented common control infrastructure;
- confirmed foreign organizational support;
- platform attribution to organized influence operation;
- official security-service warning supported by evidence;
- systematic multi-platform campaign;
- repeated use of deceptive personas;
- coordinated impersonation of institutions;
- forged security documents distributed at scale;
- campaign targeting ethnic fear or imminent-war narratives;
- coordinated false claims triggering real-world mobilization;
- documented foreign funding of information network;
- direct operational coordination between foreign and domestic actors;
- systematic targeting of election integrity;
- coordinated campaign to undermine security-force legitimacy;
- coordinated manipulation of a real security incident;
- rapid cross-border narrative transfer involving known network actors;
- campaign combining bots, media outlets, influencers, and closed channels;
- evidence that disinformation materially changes protest, mobilization, or public-order dynamics.

## Interpretation

HIGH-level indicators may justify:

`ESCALATING`

when:

- verified;
- materially new;
- persistent;
- linked to clear behavioral or security effects.

Do not assign HIGH merely because a narrative is politically sensitive.

---

# CRITICAL INDICATORS

Critical indicators represent acute information-enabled security risk.

Examples include:

- coordinated false warning triggering mass panic;
- fabricated attack claim causing immediate mobilization;
- false ethnic-violence narrative triggering retaliatory incidents;
- coordinated impersonation of security institutions during crisis;
- large-scale campaign directing people toward violent action;
- foreign-supported information operation linked to imminent violence;
- real-time disinformation supporting armed or organized coercive activity;
- coordinated false evacuation or emergency instructions;
- manipulated incident narrative causing multi-location unrest;
- campaign facilitating target identification for violence;
- direct information support to organized violent actors;
- major information operation synchronized with cross-border coercive action.

## Interpretation

CRITICAL requires:

- strongest available verification;
- active contradiction search;
- clear evidence of immediate or severe consequence.

CRITICAL must remain exceptional.

---

# Information activity ladder

Assess activity on the following ladder:

FALSE / MISLEADING CLAIM
→ REPEATED AMPLIFICATION
→ COORDINATED AMPLIFICATION
→ STABLE NETWORK
→ FOREIGN LINKAGE
→ MATERIAL SUPPORT
→ DIRECTION
→ BEHAVIORAL EFFECT
→ OFFLINE MOBILIZATION
→ SECURITY CONSEQUENCE

Movement upward on this ladder is more important than raw content volume.

Example:

isolated false claim
→ more reposts

may remain low-level.

Example:

isolated false claim
→ synchronized network amplification
→ protest mobilization

may represent escalation.

# Escalation mechanisms

The monitor must identify the exact mechanism.

Use:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

---

## Mechanism 1 — Rumor to coordinated amplification

Isolated false claim
→ synchronized multi-account dissemination
→ increased reach and manipulation potential

---

## Mechanism 2 — Amplification to stable network

Temporary viral spread
→ recurring same-account coordination
→ increased persistence and campaign capability

---

## Mechanism 3 — Domestic narrative to foreign reinforcement

Local narrative
→ systematic amplification by external actors
→ increased reach, persistence, or legitimacy

---

## Mechanism 4 — Foreign linkage to material support

Ideological alignment
→ documented financing, infrastructure, or organizational assistance
→ increased operational capability

---

## Mechanism 5 — Online narrative to offline mobilization

Escalatory false claim
→ calls for physical gathering
→ increased confrontation risk

---

## Mechanism 6 — Incident manipulation to retaliation

Real incident
→ false ethnic or political framing
→ retaliatory mobilization risk

---

## Mechanism 7 — Narrative targeting to institutional distrust

General criticism
→ systematic false claims against security institutions
→ reduced crisis-response credibility

---

## Mechanism 8 — Cross-platform coordination

Single-platform activity
→ synchronized dissemination across media, social networks, and closed channels
→ increased resilience and audience penetration

---

## Mechanism 9 — Foreign narrative to domestic adoption

External narrative
→ repeated adoption by domestic actors
→ increased local political or social effect

This alone does not prove foreign control.

---

## Mechanism 10 — Information operation to security consequence

Coordinated manipulation
→ behavioral change
→ real-world incident, panic, mobilization, or violence

If no clear mechanism exists:

do not force escalation.

# Primary evidence requirement

Assessment must be driven mainly by:

`PRIMARY ASSESSMENT EVIDENCE`

Examples:

- authenticated content;
- documented posting sequence;
- account-network evidence;
- technical infrastructure link;
- official platform action;
- financial documentation;
- organizational linkage;
- verified cross-platform coordination;
- measurable behavioral consequence;
- documented offline mobilization.

The following normally remain:

`SUPPORTING CONTEXT`

unless they materially change the assessment:

- ideological similarity;
- political alignment;
- foreign media citation;
- repeated commentary;
- general anti-Western or anti-Russian rhetoric;
- increased article volume;
- anonymous speculation.

# Falsity discipline

Before labeling content:

`DISINFORMATION`

distinguish:

- FALSE
- MISLEADING
- UNVERIFIED
- OUT OF CONTEXT
- MANIPULATED
- SATIRE
- OPINION
- PREDICTION
- DISPUTED CLAIM

Disinformation generally implies deliberate deception.

Do not infer intent merely because content is false.

When deliberate intent is unproven, use:

- false claim;
- misleading claim;
- unverified claim;
- manipulated content.

---

# Misinformation vs disinformation discipline

Distinguish:

`MISINFORMATION`
=
false information spread without demonstrated deceptive intent

from:

`DISINFORMATION`
=
deliberately deceptive information activity

When intent is unknown:

do not automatically use `DISINFORMATION`.

---

# Coordination discipline

Do not infer coordination from:

- similar opinions;
- same news event;
- same hashtag alone;
- same political position;
- same language;
- same article link.

Look for:

- timing;
- repetition;
- shared text;
- common assets;
- recurring actors;
- common infrastructure;
- sequencing;
- administrative overlap;
- task specialization;
- sustained interaction.

---

# Network evidence discipline

A network may be indicated by:

- recurring account interaction;
- stable amplification roles;
- common administrators;
- shared domains;
- coordinated links;
- common infrastructure;
- repeated synchronized behavior;
- cross-platform identity connections.

Do not infer a network merely from:

several accounts discussing the same topic.

---

# Foreign attribution discipline

Foreign attribution requires evidence.

Possible levels include:

- FOREIGN ORIGIN
- FOREIGN AMPLIFICATION
- FOREIGN CONTACT
- FOREIGN ORGANIZATIONAL LINK
- FOREIGN MATERIAL SUPPORT
- FOREIGN DIRECTION
- FOREIGN CONTROL

These are not interchangeable.

Example:

Russian outlet publishes narrative
→ FOREIGN ORIGIN

Domestic actors repeat it
→ DOMESTIC ADOPTION

This does not automatically prove:

FOREIGN DIRECTION

---

# Foreign-link evidence

Relevant evidence may include:

- financing;
- contracts;
- organizational ownership;
- staff overlap;
- technical infrastructure;
- recurring coordination;
- training;
- instructions;
- internal communications;
- official attribution;
- documented intermediary structure.

Do not infer linkage from:

- nationality;
- language;
- ideology;
- embassy contact;
- one meeting;
- one repost.

---

# Platform behavior discipline

Distinguish:

- high posting frequency;
- automated behavior;
- bot-like behavior;
- confirmed automation;
- coordinated inauthentic behavior.

Do not call an account:

`BOT`

without sufficient evidence.

Use cautious wording:

- „акаунт с признаци на автоматизирано поведение“
- „неавтентично координирано поведение“
- „няма потвърждение, че профилът е автоматизиран“

---

# Telegram discipline

Telegram may provide valuable early signals.

But distinguish:

- original claim;
- forwarded claim;
- anonymous allegation;
- channel commentary;
- verified primary statement;
- coordinated dissemination.

For significant Telegram signals:

- trace earliest available origin;
- check timestamps;
- identify repost chain;
- search open web;
- search official sources;
- preserve uncertainty.

Telegram volume is not proof of real-world scale.

---

# Media ecosystem discipline

Several media outlets may form:

- independent reporting chain;
- common agency chain;
- partisan amplification chain;
- coordinated network.

Ask:

- Who originated the claim?
- Who copied whom?
- Is there original reporting?
- Are headlines based on same statement?
- Is the same article syndicated?

Do not count copies as independent evidence.

---

- deepfake;
- AI-generated audio;
- manipulated video;

`AI-GENERATED`

# Behavioral effect discipline

Distinguish:

- EXPOSURE
- ENGAGEMENT
- BELIEF
- ATTITUDE CHANGE
- BEHAVIORAL INTENT
- OFFLINE ACTION

High views do not prove behavioral effect.

A campaign becomes more security-relevant when evidence shows:

- protest mobilization;
- threats;
- panic;
- avoidance behavior;
- retaliatory action;
- institutional disruption;
- violence.

---

# Reach discipline

Do not equate:

- follower count;
- views;
- reposts;

with:

- unique audience;
- persuasion;
- influence;
- behavioral impact.

Treat platform metrics cautiously.

---

# Official attribution discipline

An official claim of foreign interference is important.

But distinguish:

- political accusation;
- preliminary assessment;
- intelligence assessment;
- formal investigation;
- public evidence;
- judicial evidence.

Do not upgrade one into another.

---

# Fact-check discipline

Fact-checking may establish:

- falsity;
- manipulation;
- context.

It may not establish:

- coordination;
- foreign direction;
- campaign control.

Do not treat a debunked claim as proof of an influence operation.

# De-escalation indicators

Possible de-escalation indicators include:

- campaign loses amplification;
- coordinated network disrupted;
- platform removes network;
- false claim corrected before wider spread;
- key amplifiers retract;
- no offline behavioral effect emerges;
- authorities provide credible clarification;
- audience engagement sharply declines;
- network infrastructure becomes inactive;
- foreign support is disrupted;
- mobilization calls fail to produce action;
- competing accurate information reduces panic.

A decline in article volume alone does not prove de-escalation.

---

# Stabilization indicators

Possible stabilization indicators include:

- false narrative remains isolated;
- no stable network appears;
- no cross-platform spread;
- no foreign linkage is demonstrated;
- no offline mobilization occurs;
- suspected coordination remains unconfirmed;
- campaign does not expand geographically;
- public institutions maintain information credibility.

These may support:

`STABLE`

or:

`INSUFFICIENT DATA`

# Evidence requirements

For LOW-level indicators:

- credible discovery source may be sufficient;
- original-source tracing required where possible.

For MEDIUM-level indicators:

- seek repeated behavioral evidence;
- network evidence;
- independent corroboration;
- primary content archive.

For HIGH-level indicators:

- seek technical, platform, official, financial, organizational, or strong investigative evidence;
- perform contradiction search.

For CRITICAL indicators:

- use highest available verification standard;
- actively test:
  - origin;
  - timing;
  - actor identity;
  - coordination;
  - foreign linkage;
  - behavioral effect;
  - offline consequence.

---

Prioritize where relevant:

1. Original content
2. Platform transparency reports
3. Official security or prosecutorial evidence
4. Court documentation

Source role matters.

`CONFIRMATION`

→ possible confirmation

# Daily assessment decision rules

## STABLE

Use when:

- false or manipulative content exists;
- no verified increase in coordination, capability, foreign linkage, or behavioral effect appears;
- activity remains within normal information competition.

---

## LIMITED ESCALATION

Use when:

- at least one verified development materially increases network capability, coordination, persistence, reach, or security relevance;
- a clear escalation mechanism is demonstrated;
- scope remains limited.

Before assigning, state internally:

PREVIOUS CONDITION
→ NEW DEVELOPMENT
→ CHANGED RISK

Example:

isolated false narrative
→ synchronized amplification by recurring account cluster
→ increased manipulation and mobilization potential

---

## ESCALATING

Use when:

- several primary indicators worsen;
- coordination increases;
- campaign scope expands;
- foreign support strengthens;
- cross-platform activity grows;
- behavioral or offline consequences emerge.

---

## MIXED

Use only when:

- one capability dimension worsens;
- another genuinely weakens.

Do not use MIXED merely because true and false information coexist.

---

## INSUFFICIENT DATA

Use when:

- coordination is speculative;
- foreign attribution depends on ideology alone;
- account identity is unclear;
- source chains are weak;
- behavioral effect cannot be demonstrated.

---

# Significance discipline

Do not confuse:

`SIGNIFICANCE`

with:

`ESCALATION`

or:

`CONFIDENCE`

Example:

A false claim about imminent war may be:

HIGH potential significance
+
LOW confidence regarding coordination

It must not automatically produce:

ESCALATING

Example:

A small but verified coordinated network may be:

MEDIUM significance
+
LIMITED ESCALATION

# Watchlist requirements

Watch indicators must be:

- specific;
- observable;
- technically or procedurally feasible;
- linked to a defined mechanism.

Good examples:

- whether the same account cluster repeats synchronized posting;
- whether an original false claim migrates from Telegram to mainstream media;
- whether platform operators remove linked accounts;
- whether a named foreign organization materially supports the network;
- whether identical media assets appear across additional accounts;
- whether mobilization messaging produces physical gatherings;
- whether a competent authority issues a documented attribution;
- whether a fabricated ethnic-incident claim triggers retaliatory activity.

Bad examples:
